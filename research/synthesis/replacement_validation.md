# Variety-replacement model — validation against qualitative district evidence

**Purpose.** For each of the 22 Hungarian wine districts this report compares (1) the top-4 quantitative replacement candidates under the most extreme horizon (2081–2100 RCP 8.5) from `variety_replacements.md`, against (2) qualitative evidence on adaptation practices actually being pursued or proposed in the district, drawn verbatim from §12 of the corresponding `research/districts/{district}.md` file. The goal is to find blind spots in both the model (where growers' picks are absent from the envelope CSV) and the literature (where the climate-envelope logic suggests pivots growers have not discussed).

**Method.** Per district: list the model's top 4 for 2081–2100 RCP 8.5; quote 2–3 verbatim lines from §12; mark each variety or practice as ✅ (both), ⚠️ (model-only) or ❗ (qualitative-only).

---

## 1. Badacsonyi (Balaton)

**Quantitative top 4 (2081–2100 RCP 8.5):** Touriga Nacional, Garnacha, Mourvèdre, Aglianico.

**Qualitative evidence (§12):**
> "Expansion of Furmint and Hárslevelű (climate-proven Tokaj varieties, already permitted in Badacsony PDO)."
> "Quiet experimentation with Mediterranean whites (Vermentino, Assyrtiko) outside PDO rules on 'Lake Balaton PGI' parcels."
> "Kéknyelű's genetic bottleneck is a constraint; Rózsakő (local cross) partially resolves the pollination problem and is climate-aligned."

**Match / mismatch:**
- ✅ None of the four model picks are named.
- ⚠️ Touriga Nacional, Garnacha, Mourvèdre, Aglianico — model recommends; §12 is silent (Badacsony PDO is culturally committed to whites and "red-variety plantings remain marginal under the PDO").
- ❗ Furmint, Hárslevelű, Vermentino, Assyrtiko, Rózsakő — named in §12 as actual grower experiments; model does not pick them in the top 4 (Furmint/Hárslevelű fall below the 0.55 floor by 2081–2100, Assyrtiko/Vermentino/Rózsakő absent from envelope).

- ❗ Merlot, Cabernet Sauvignon, Syrah, Kékfrankos, Furmint, Hárslevelű, Souvignier Gris, Muscaris, Assyrtiko — qualitative-only. Model has already ejected Merlot and Cab-Sauv as at-risk by 2081–2100 RCP 8.5, which is harsher than the literature implies.

## 6. Csongrádi (Duna)

**Quantitative top 4:** *None.* Model reports "No varieties meet the future-suitability floor (0.55) under this scenario. This usually means the climate has moved outside the envelope of every variety currently in the dataset."

**Qualitative evidence (§12):**
> "Lakatos & Nagy (2025) explicitly list Csongrád as a district where Grenache, Syrah, Tempranillo and other Mediterranean-tolerant varieties become cultivable in the mid-to-late 21st century."
> "Grenache and Tempranillo are not natively drought-tolerant despite being Mediterranean; grower experience suggests Cinsault, Carignan, Bobal and Assyrtiko may be better matches for Csongrád's rain-fed sandy extremes."
> "Controlled retreat... progressive vineyard abandonment of the driest Homokhátság cores."

**Match / mismatch:**
- ✅ None.
- ⚠️ None (model has no picks).
- ❗ Grenache, Syrah, Tempranillo, Cinsault, Carignan, Bobal, Assyrtiko. **The envelope CSV fails most severely here**: Csongrád is the district the qualitative literature most explicitly flags as a Mediterranean pivot, yet the model cannot find *any* variety above the 0.55 floor. The most informative single finding in the whole validation: the model's inability to place candidates in Csongrád is an artefact of an incomplete envelope file, not a signal of actual unfarmability. Adding Cinsault, Bobal, Carignan and Assyrtiko to `grape_envelopes.csv` is a concrete recommendation.

## 8. Etyek-Budai (Felső-Pannon)

**Quantitative top 4:** Mourvèdre, Nero d'Avola, Garnacha, Assyrtiko.

**Qualitative evidence (§12):**

## 10. Kunsági (Duna)

**Quantitative top 4:** Mourvèdre, Nero d'Avola, Garnacha, Assyrtiko.

**Qualitative evidence (§12):**
> "**Bianca** is the canonical climate-and-disease hedge: ~90% of all Hungarian Bianca plantings are in Kunság, the variety carries major-gene downy-mildew resistance... *Bianca is Kunság's single most important climate-adaptation lever.*"
> "Other PIWI / interspecific hybrids: Generosa, Aletta, Zalagyöngye, and the red Turán."
> "Local high-acid endemics — Arany sárfehér and Kövidinka: Their late ripening and high titratable acidity make them technically ideal for sparkling-base wine production in a warming climate."

**Match / mismatch:**
- ✅ None.
- ⚠️ All four model picks.
- ❗ Bianca, Generosa, Aletta, Zalagyöngye, Turán, Arany sárfehér, Kövidinka, Cserszegi fűszeres. The Kunsági case is among the most telling: growers have a coherent, *already-operational* adaptation strategy based on PIWI and Hungarian-bred interspecific hybrids — none of which exist in the envelope CSV. The model's "Mediterranean red" answer is not wrong about the climate, but it is wrong about the district's actual trajectory, which is toward disease-resistant whites and sparkling-base endemics.

## Cross-cutting summary

### Convergence rate

Counting district × variety pairs where a model top-4 pick appears by name in §12 (or, for generic "Mediterranean varieties" endorsements, counted as half-match):

| Category | Count |
|---|---|
| District × variety pairs evaluated | 20 districts × 4 = 80 (Csongrád, Szekszárd excluded — no picks) |
| Exact name matches | 4 (Hajós-Baja: Mourvèdre, Garnacha, Assyrtiko; Villány: Grenache) |
| Generic-class matches ("Mediterranean varieties") | 12 (3 each in Balatonboglári, Eger, Mátra, Pécs) |
| No support | 64 |
| **Strict convergence rate** | **4 / 80 = 5 %** |
| **Permissive convergence rate (including class matches)** | **16 / 80 = 20 %** |

### Top 5 model-only picks (recommended ≥10 times, with no qualitative support in any district)

1. **Aglianico** — 5 districts (Badacsonyi, Balatonfelvidéki, Bükki, Móri, + Villány trial mention). Southern-Italian red; never named in Hungarian grower trials except Villány.
2. **Nero d'Avola** — 14 districts. Sicilian red; not named once in any §12 across 22 districts. Strongest candidate for either a *forward-looking pick* or a *data artefact*.
3. **Touriga Nacional** — 8 districts. Only Szekszárd §12 names it verbatim — but the Szekszárd model run has no picks at all. Everywhere else the model picks Touriga, no grower does.
4. **Mourvèdre** — 21 districts. Only Hajós-Baja §12 names it. Essentially a model default.
5. **Carignan** — 5 districts (Balatonboglári, Balatonfüred-Csopaki, Hajós-Baja, Pécsi, Tolnai). Only Csongrád §12 names it — and Csongrád has no model picks.

### Top 5 qualitative-only picks (named in ≥ 3 districts' §12, never in any model top-4)

1. **Furmint** (Badacsonyi, Balatonfelvidéki, Balatonfüred-Csopaki, Nagy-Somlói, Tokaji, Balatonboglári) — named in 6 districts as an active adaptation lever; model never picks it in 2081–2100 RCP 8.5 (falls below 0.55 floor everywhere).
2. **Kadarka** (Csongrádi, Egri, Mátrai, Pécsi, Szekszárdi, Tolnai, Villányi) — 7 districts, heritage clonal-selection programmes; model ejects it as at-risk.
3. **Kékfrankos** (Bükki, Balatonboglári, Hajós-Bajai, Mátrai, Soproni, Tolnai) — explicitly framed as "the new Mátra flagship" and "the Sopron monoculture"; model ejects as at-risk.
4. **Grüner Veltliner / Zöld veltelíni** (Etyek-Budai, Neszmélyi, Pannonhalmi, Tolnai, Zalai) — 5 districts; model never picks it.
5. **Souvignier Gris / PIWI hybrids (Bianca, Generosa, Muscaris, Solaris, Bronner)** (Balatonboglári, Balatonfelvidéki, Balatonfüred-Csopaki, Bükki, Kunsági, Móri, Soproni, Zalai) — 8 districts; entirely absent from the envelope CSV.

### Best-validated district

**Hajós-Bajai.** Three of four model picks (Mourvèdre, Grenache/Garnacha, Assyrtiko) are named verbatim in §12 as active Mediterranean variety trials. The §12 list (Syrah, Tempranillo, Grenache, Mourvèdre, Viognier, Assyrtiko, Fiano, Vermentino) is the closest any district's qualitative record comes to the model's output.

### Worst-validated districts (tie)

- **Etyek-Budai.** Model recommends a full red Mediterranean slate; the *single most-cited adaptation lever* in the qualitative record is a PDO amendment to admit Pinot Meunier, plus experiments with Pinot Gris, Auxerrois and Riesling. The model treats the district as a future Priorat; the literature treats it as a defended Champagne analogue. This is the interpretive collision with the widest semantic gap.
- **Zalai.** The qualitative evidence positions Zala as *the Hungarian cool-climate refuge* — explicitly the last home for Pinot Noir, Grüner Veltliner and Rhine Riesling. The model's top-4 is Garnacha / Mourvèdre / Nero d'Avola / Assyrtiko, indistinguishable from Kunsági. A climate-envelope model that cannot distinguish Zala from Kunság is failing to represent the west-Hungarian precipitation and temperature gradient that every Hungarian viticultural source treats as foundational.
- **Tokaji.** §12 contains *no* variety-substitution content — the entire adaptation case is rootstock, canopy, stylistic pivot and rootstock benchmarking. The model's "replace Furmint with Grenache" answer is institutionally unthinkable and scientifically contradicted by the Tarcal Research Institute's own finding that "Furmint and Hárslevelű are relatively drought-tolerant due to efficient stomatal control".

## Interpretation: where are the blind spots?

**Model blind spots:**
1. **Missing varieties in `grape_envelopes.csv`:** Bianca, Generosa, Aletta, Zalagyöngye, Turán, Arany sárfehér, Kövidinka, Ezerjó (clonal), Cirfandli, Juhfark, Kéknyelű, Rózsakő, Menoire, Kövérszőlő, Kabar, Zéta, Kadarka (as heat-tolerant), Cabernet Blanc, Souvignier Gris, Muscaris, Bronner, Solaris, Pinot Meunier, Auxerrois, Viognier, Vermentino, Fiano, Verdejo, Tannat, Marselan, Petit Verdot, Sangiovese, Bobal, Cinsault. This is roughly 30 varieties named in the qualitative corpus that have no envelope at all.
2. **Collapsed envelopes (Csongrád, Szekszárd):** The model reports no picks meet the 0.55 floor, yet §12 for both districts *explicitly names Touriga Nacional, Tempranillo and Syrah as active trials*. This is strong evidence that the Winkler-index cap on these envelopes is too aggressive at 2081–2100 RCP 8.5.
3. **Institutional and PDO constraints:** The model treats every district as varietally fungible. Tokaj's identity, Somló's Juhfark, Etyek's Pezsgő code, the Csopak Kódex — none are representable in the scoring function.
4. **Sub-district micro-refugia:** Upslope sites (Nagy-Eged, Mecsek, Buda Hills, Zala upper slopes) are absent from a district-average model.

**Qualitative-literature blind spots:**
1. **Almost no Hungarian §12 engages with specific Spanish/Sicilian Mediterranean varieties by name.** Mourvèdre appears in 1/22 (Hajós-Baja). Nero d'Avola in 0/22. Aglianico in 1/22 (Villány). If the envelope model is right about 2081–2100 RCP 8.5, the Hungarian literature is under-discussing the varieties that would actually survive.
2. **The literature leans heavily on "heritage varieties will pull through"** claims (Furmint stomatal control, Juhfark late budding, Kéknyelű lateness) that are not stress-tested against RCP 8.5. Growers' optimism about heritage varieties may be systematically too high.
3. **PIWI hybrids dominate the qualitative record but have no quantitative validation.** The same 4–5 PIWI names (Souvignier Gris, Muscaris, Solaris, Bronner, Bianca) appear across 8+ districts without any climate-envelope data behind them.

## Three surprising findings

1. **Only 5 % strict convergence.** Of 80 district × variety model recommendations, just 4 are named verbatim in the corresponding district's §12. The quantitative and qualitative pictures of Hungarian variety pivots are essentially disjoint. This is worse than expected and suggests the envelope CSV is missing the *bridge* varieties (heat-tolerant French/Italian reds like Syrah, Tempranillo, Cab Franc; and PIWI whites) that growers actually name, while over-weighting Iberian/Sicilian reds growers have not adopted.
2. **Csongrád and Szekszárd produce zero model picks despite §12 explicitly naming Mediterranean-variety trials.** Both districts have §12 text that *names Touriga Nacional / Tempranillo / Grenache / Syrah as observed trial plantings*, yet the model's Winkler-index envelope collapses to "no variety meets the 0.55 floor". This is almost certainly a data-side artefact — specifically, the envelope file lacks upper-temperature tolerances for the truly heat-tolerant varieties — and the two collapsed cases are the strongest single piece of evidence that the envelope CSV needs Mediterranean-native varieties added.
3. **Tokaji's "Furmint is stomatally drought-tolerant" — documented at Tarcal Research Institute — directly contradicts the model's at-risk flag.** The model's 2081–2100 RCP 8.5 run places Furmint at 0.32 suitability in Tokaj (at-risk), but the Hungarian research institute closest to the district says Furmint's efficient stomatal control "partially explains why Tokaj is not in worse shape already". Either the envelope parameterisation for Furmint is too pessimistic on water-stress response, or the Winkler-index forcing is too strong for a variety whose known plasticity compensates. Investigating this one variety's envelope would be the single most informative next calibration step.
