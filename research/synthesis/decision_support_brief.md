# Climate Change Adaptation in Hungarian Wine Districts
## A Decision-Support Brief for Growers, Winery Managers and Policy Makers

**Prepared for:** Hegyközségek Nemzeti Tanácsa (HNT), the Ministry of Agriculture, OMSZ/HungaroMet, MATE, the Eszterházy Károly Catholic University (Eger) wine-climate group, EU CAP advisors, and the working growers of all 22 Hungarian borvidék.

**Status:** Synthesis of 22 district-level climate-viticulture briefs held in `C:\Bor-szőlő\research\districts\`. English primary, bilingual glossary in Appendix A. No external research beyond the 22 source files.

**Scope:** Action-oriented. Where the evidence is contested, it is marked contested. Where the evidence is missing, it is marked GAP.

---

## 1. Executive summary

Hungarian viticulture is already living through a climate regime that has no historical analogue in the Pannonian Basin. The unifying finding of the Lakatos & Nagy (2025) assessment of all 22 Hungarian wine regions is that every district has crossed from its 1971–2000 Winkler/Huglin class into a distinctly warmer one, and that under RCP8.5 the end-of-century growing-season warming is expected to reach **+2.44 °C (Sopron) to +2.96 °C (Tokaj)**, with Csongrád exceeding **+2.5 °C** and standing out as Hungary's maximum-warming district. Under the pessimistic single-model tails, growing-season warming exceeds **+4 °C** across much of the country. Counter-intuitively, the northern and eastern districts (Tokaj, Eger, Bükk, Mátra) warm **faster** in absolute GDD / Huglin terms than the traditionally warmer south — the national thermal gradient is flattening.

Three things follow.

**First, the existential-risk districts** — those where the literature forces an explicit question of whether the PDO as historically constituted remains agronomically meaningful — are:
- **Csongrádi** (worst case: flat sand, no elevation buffer, fastest warming, worst drought trend, weakest adaptive capacity, smallest grower margins).
- **Kunsági** (Hungary's largest district by area; sitting on the Homokhátság aridification front with 2–5 m groundwater drop since the 1970s; bulk-wine economics leave no capital for adaptation).
- **Hajós-Bajai** (Alföld loess ridge; projected into Winkler V with no modern Hungarian analogue).
- **Nagy-Somlói** (tiny footprint, no migration buffer, identity fused to a single cool-climate variety — Juhfark).
- **Etyek-Budai** (Hungary's sparkling-wine base; a *pure downside* climate exposure because the entire product rests on coolness, with no stylistic "upside" interpretation of warming available).

**Second, the transition districts** — where the wine style and grape portfolio can survive but must be actively rewritten, and where adaptation investment is most leveraged — are:
- **Badacsonyi, Balaton-felvidéki, Balatonboglári, Balatonfüred-Csopaki** (the four largest Balaton PDOs) — all facing compounded lake-mesoclimate, tourism and stylistic exposure.
- **Tokaji** — classical Aszú vintages becoming rarer, dry Furmint/Hárslevelű now ~1/3 of harvest and rising; paradoxically the fastest-warming *northern* district in absolute index change.
- **Egri, Bükki, Mátrai** — Upper Hungarian red-and-aromatic-white cluster where warming unlocks Mediterranean reds but erodes the cool-climate white and Pinot Noir heritage.
- **Szekszárdi, Villányi, Tolnai, Pécsi** — Pannon red-wine belt transitioning from Bordeaux-analogue into Languedoc/Rhône-analogue; Kadarka under pressure, Cabernet Franc and Syrah gaining.
- **Móri, Neszmélyi, Pannonhalmi** — smaller cool-white districts where near-term is manageable but varietal identity is on the line.

**Third, the provisional climate "winners"** — districts explicitly flagged as among Hungary's slowest-warming in Lakatos & Nagy (2025) and the Klímapolitikai Intézet (2023) analysis — are:
- **Soproni** (smallest increase on all four viticultural indices in the near-future window; the only Hungarian district positioned internationally as a "climate refuge").
- **Zalai** (Hungary's wettest district, Atlantic-influenced, now positioned as a cool-climate Pinot Noir/Grüner Veltliner/Riesling refuge).
- **Pannonhalmi** (explicitly identified by Lakatos & Nagy as projected to warm *least* of the 22 — +2.3 °C under RCP8.5, vs national mean +2.71 °C).

These verdicts are all provisional. Under the pessimistic RCP8.5 tail even Sopron crosses from Winkler I through II into Region III/IV.

### Top 5 highest-leverage adaptation actions Hungary as a whole should take

1. **Ease the legal restrictions on vineyard irrigation.** Called for unanimously across the Tokaji, Egri, Kunsági, Csongrádi, Hajós-Bajai, Villányi and Móri files. Without this lever the fastest-warming districts have no credible adaptation pathway, and continuing to forbid it effectively writes Csongrád and parts of Kunság off the PDO map.
2. **Revise PDO boundaries, variety lists and chemistry/yield caps district by district.** Lakatos & Nagy (2025) explicitly recommend this. The Csopak Kódex (private voluntary code for Balatonfüred-Csopaki) and the Villányi Franc 2014 rules are the domestic templates — both were written as climate-resilience protocols even before the word was used.
3. **Publish the Lakatos & Nagy / FORESEE 4.0 supplementary tables at per-PDO resolution.** The single most requested missing data product across all 22 district files. Every downstream planning, insurance, zoning and PDO-reform decision in the country currently relies on a paper whose per-district numbers are not published.
4. **Establish district-level phenology and pest-monitoring networks** (budburst → flowering → véraison → harvest; *Scaphoideus titanus*, *Halyomorpha halys*, *Drosophila suzukii*, Flavescence dorée, esca). Only Tokaj and a handful of Eger/Sopron plots currently have anything resembling multi-decadal series; 20 of 22 districts do not.
5. **Fund coupled lake–vineyard mesoclimate modelling for the four Balaton PDOs** and the Fertő-tó shoreline of Sopron. EURO-CORDEX 0.11° (~12 km) does not resolve Lake Balaton's ~594 km² thermal buffer or its projected Zala-basin runoff collapse (~40% decline by 2071–2100), which is the physical lever on which the entire Balaton wine-tourism economy depends.

## 3. District-by-district 3-line briefs

Each brief: (a) one-sentence climate verdict; (b) one-sentence biggest threat; (c) one-sentence highest-leverage adaptation action.

### Badacsonyi
- Near-term "golden age" for Kéknyelű on lake-buffered basalt, but the district is at the edge of its climate envelope and is not among Hungary's climate winners.
- The compound threat is lake-mesoclimate degradation (Zala-basin runoff projected to fall ≥40% by 2071–2100) combined with sunburn risk on basalt talus and genetic uniformity of the Kéknyelű bottleneck.
- Expand Furmint, Hárslevelű and Rózsakő under the existing PDO while commissioning coupled lake–vineyard mesoclimate modelling to quantify how much of the lake-effect buffer remains.

### Balaton-felvidéki
- Káli Basin and the Tapolca witness-hills are thermally moving from Burgundy-analogue toward southern-Rhône-analogue; acidity is still preserved but "classical" lean Olaszrizling is eroding.
- Spring-frost risk on upper dolomite/rendzina plots is rising because phenology has advanced faster than the last-frost date, and hail in the Bakony orographic corridor is not yet measured at district level.
- Late pruning + clonal shift toward later-ripening Olaszrizling and Rajnai rizling, plus reactivation of abandoned north-facing Káli basin parcels as "climate insurance" sites.

### Balatonboglári
- Hungary's largest Balaton PDO by area and the south-shore volume/sparkling engine, now crossing into Winkler IV on the lakeshore loess ramp — comparable to present-day southern Rhône.
- Aromatic fresh whites (Irsai Olivér, Cserszegi fűszeres, Királyleányka, Sauvignon Blanc) and sparkling-base Chardonnay are becoming marginal on the lakeshore shelf.
- Retreat sparkling-base and aromatic-white production inland to the cooler Külső-Somogy uplands (Marcali, Gamás, Kaposvár direction) and commission a formal "climate-insurance" zoning of those parcels.

### Balatonfüred-Csopaki
- Of the six Balaton PDOs this is the district whose institutional framework — the Csopak Kódex (2013, ~20–30 producers) — is already a functioning climate-resilience protocol, and the least unprepared.
- Classical linear 12–13 % vol., >5.5 g/L TA Csopak Olaszrizling becomes structurally difficult to produce on the red-sandstone lakeshore by ~2050 under RCP8.5.
- Protect upper-slope dolomite and marl sites into a formal premium tier, retreat de facto from the hottest red-sandstone lakeshore parcels, and resist PDO dilution that would weaken the Kódex discipline.

### Bükki
- The most thermally sensitive district in Hungary in absolute terms (Lakatos & Nagy 2025: BEDD change +258.49 °C·d, the country's largest), warming from a cool baseline into a genuinely new regime.
- Chronic data poverty (most under-studied of the 22) combined with the smallest economic weight leaves the district with no capital base to respond to its own climatic acceleration.
- Leverage the Szövetség a Bükki Borvidékért (2018–19) as a collective vehicle for frost protection, terroir mapping and a deliberate "volcanic whites + mountain-fringe Kékfrankos" dual identity that rides the warming rather than resisting it.

### Csongrádi
- Hungary's **worst-case district under RCP8.5**: explicitly flagged by Lakatos & Nagy (2025) as showing the largest projected AGST rise (>2.5 °C), landing end-century AGST at ~20.0–20.5 °C — beyond the established range of any European fine-wine region.
- The combination of flat sand, zero elevation/aspect buffer, drought hotspot (Bács-Kiskun / Békés / Csongrád-Csanád were hit hardest by the 2022 drought), collapsing grower economics and smallholder structure leaves effectively no private adaptation capacity.
- The **only credible policy intervention is ease of irrigation rights plus honest controlled retreat** of the driest Homokhátság cores toward the Tisza-alluvial Csongrád-town zone, combined with a formal Mediterranean varietal pivot (Syrah, Tempranillo, Cinsault, Assyrtiko) written into the PDO.

### Egri
- Northern Hungary's flagship red district has already unlocked a Mediterranean varietal palette — Syrah, Tempranillo, Viognier in trial — while still holding its Kékfrankos-and-Kadarka cool-climate freshness identity on Nagy-Eged and other high sites.
- The Eger valley basin and Verpelét/Feldebrő flats remain frost-prone (2007, 2011, 2017, 2020, 2021, 2024) precisely because earlier budburst outpaces the receding last-frost date.
- Continue to deploy the Pernesz et al. / Misik et al. late-pruning and post-véraison apical-defoliation protocol district-wide, and defend Nagy-Eged and the upper 400 m sites as strategic cool-climate refuges for Pinot Noir and Bikavér Grand Superior.

### Etyek-Budai
- Hungary's sparkling-wine heartland is the **only district in which climate warming is unambiguously a pure downside** — there is no stylistic interpretation of warming that benefits sparkling base-wine production (Törley sources ~810 ha here, ~65–70 % of Hungarian sparkling volume).
- Chardonnay/Pinot Noir base-wine acidity, pH and potential-alcohol are all moving in the wrong direction; pick windows have already shifted from late September to mid-August.
- The clearest single lever is a PDO amendment permitting **Pinot Meunier** (later-budding, better acid retention), alongside higher-elevation plantings on the Buda Hills (~310 m) and Velence Hills, and slower-ripening Dijon Chardonnay clones (95, 96).

### Hajós-Bajai
- Southern Alföld loess ridge district projected under RCP8.5 end-century to reach HI ~2,700–3,000 (HI+3 "warm" to "very warm") and Winkler V — a climate with **no modern Hungarian analogue**.
- Groundwater decline of 2–5 m (locally up to 10 m) across the Homokhátság since the 1970s directly threatens the loess-gravel rootzone, and ice-wine / late-harvest categories are already failing reliability.
- Consolidate a "Swabian Cabernet + Cserszegi fűszeres + siller" warm-climate identity, preserve the Hajósi Pincefalu (~1,200 loess cellars) as passive thermal infrastructure, and plant heat-tolerant rootstocks (110R, 140Ru, 1103P) on all new blocks.

### Kunsági
- Hungary's largest wine district and its most vulnerable on five axes simultaneously (fastest observed GDD rise, lowest baseline precipitation, strongest aridification, lowest soil water-holding capacity, thinnest economic margin) — Lakatos & Nagy (2025) report **+581.65 °C·d GDD change, the largest of any Hungarian region**.
- The bulk-wine margin is so thin that private adaptation investment is essentially impossible, and private irrigation from shallow wells is simultaneously the only effective on-farm buffer *and* the proximate cause of the regional groundwater commons collapse.
- **Bianca is Kunság's single most important climate-adaptation lever** — ~90 % of Hungarian Bianca plantings are in the district already — and deserves explicit policy support as the centrepiece of a rebuilt PDO identity around resistant varieties (Bianca, Generosa, Aletta, Zalagyöngye, Turán) and sparkling base from Arany sárfehér and Kövidinka.

### Mátrai
- Hungary's second-largest district by area, warming on the northern gradient but with **genuine vertical adaptation headroom** (Sár-hegy ~500 m, Verpelét-adjacent parcels, higher Mátra foothill terraces).
- Harvest windows have already collapsed: traditional "never before the last Sunday of October" has moved to mid-September, with 2024 forcing simultaneous multi-variety ripening.
- Systematic upslope migration to 400–500 m plus expansion of heat-adapted reds (Kékfrankos, Kadarka revival, Syrah, Tempranillo, Gamay) — Mátra is explicitly in the "altitude-adaptation-capable cohort" of Hungarian districts.

### Móri
- The single Hungarian district with the **highest projected water deficit** in Lakatos & Nagy (2025), despite a moderate temperature signal — a temperature-positive but water-negative trajectory.
- Ezerjó (hungarikum-listed, ~one-fifth to one-third of district area) is losing its cool-climate refuge and, in parallel, the Móri-szél wind regime that historically protected it has no published trend analysis and may be weakening under the European "stilling" signal.
- Start a dedicated Ezerjó clonal selection / breeding programme for late-budburst, slow-sugar, high-acid biotypes (no such programme currently exists) and install a proper meteorological station inside the Móri-árok.

### Nagy-Somlói
- Hungary's smallest (~500 ha) and most identity-vulnerable district: the literature is unanimous that Juhfark's cool-climate austere style is at the edge of climatic viability, and there is no migration buffer within the footprint.
- The specific threat is loss of *typicity* — malic-acid retention, "gun-flinty" minerality, austere structure — which directly triggers a 30–50 % price reset on analogies with warmer volcanic whites.
- Deploy north-aspect plantings on Somló-hegy, Ság-hegy and Kis-Somló as the district's only cool-climate refuge, and fund a dedicated Somló climate-viticulture monitoring programme (the highest-value single data gap in Hungarian viticulture).

### Neszmélyi
- One of the four or five least-warming Hungarian districts, but the structural problem is that Hilltop Neszmély (Törley-adjacent in scale, ~30 % of the district and ~7–8 million bottles/year mostly for export) is committed to a "light, fresh, fragrant" aromatic-white identity that warming directly erodes.
- No margin for "letting the wines get bigger" without losing the UK/Scandinavia supermarket positioning that underwrites the district's commercial model.
- Double down on later-budding aromatic varieties (Cserszegi fűszeres above all, plus Grüner Veltliner), prioritise cooler north-aspect and Danube-facing blocks, and build a Bertalan-Balázs-style erosion-control cover-cropping programme district-wide.

### Pannonhalmi
- Hungary's projected slowest-warming district (+2.3 °C under RCP8.5 — Lakatos & Nagy 2025 explicit finding), and one of the literature's few named "climate refuges" inside Hungary.
- Even in the slowest-warming district the wines are becoming "noticeably fuller-bodied and more alcoholic in the last ten years", so the identity conversation is already live.
- Consolidate Riesling as the Abbey's long-horizon flagship, keep Pinot Noir as the premium red while it is still viable, and leverage the UNESCO monastic brand + Liptai Zsolt's national profile into an export-facing "Hungarian climate refuge" proposition.

### Pécsi
- A genuine paradox: the PTE research institute winemaker István Ipacs Szabó has quantified that **Cirfandli now produces outstanding wine in 6 of 10 vintages vs 2 of 10 under the classical regime** — a clear warming benefit for a late-ripening indigenous variety.
- But Cirfandli area has collapsed **40 % in 5 years to just 13.9 ha (2024)**, the district sits in one of Hungary's three fastest-warming sub-regions (WB-CCKP flags the Mecsek), and Cirfandli is the district's only globally-rare identity asset.
- **Conserve Cirfandli through the PTE gene bank (world's 6th largest, ~1,500 accessions)**, reposition the variety toward late-harvest and off-dry styles where warming is an asset, and plant new blocks at higher-elevation Mecsek footslope sites (300–440 m, Hosszúhetény–Pécsvárad direction).

### Soproni
- Hungary's slowest-warming district on all four indices (AGST, GDD, HI, BEDD), with Lake Fertő buffering, sub-Alpine position and NW Atlantic advection, and already marketed internationally as a "climate refuge".
- Even here the 1991–2020 warming is +1.97 °C and 9 of the 10 hottest heatwaves on record are post-2000, so "slowest warming" is only "slowest" in relative terms.
- Build a cross-border Hungarian-Austrian Kékfrankos / Blaufränkisch climate strategy with Mittelburgenland DAC as an integrated premium-red region of continental significance, and formalise the "Hungarian climate refuge" export positioning.

### Szekszárdi
- Above the Hungarian average in projected climate-exposure increment (RegCM +1.61, ALADIN +2.03; Pieczka et al. 2021), with Kadarka, Merlot and classical Bikavér all under pressure.
- The single biggest identity issue is that Szekszárdi Bikavér currently **requires ≥5 % Kadarka** by PDO rule — the variety is the brand's distinguishing guarantor vs Egri Bikavér — but Kadarka is the earliest variety to leave its safe operating space under warming.
- Continue and expand the Heimann & Fiai Kadarka clonal programme (smaller berries, looser clusters, thicker skins) and formally permit heat-tolerant Mediterranean reds (Syrah, Tempranillo, Touriga Nacional) in the Bikavér ≤10 % minor-variety slot.

### Tokaji
- Hungary's fastest-warming northern district in absolute index terms (Lakatos & Nagy 2025: AGST change +2.96 °C, the country's highest; BEDD change +60.02 °C·d, also the highest), with early-August harvests now common and the classical Aszú-vintage reliability eroding.
- Dry Furmint / Hárslevelű already makes up ~1/3 of the harvest, but PDO identity and four decades of international legal defence of the Tokaj name are built on aszú — and Austria is now planting Furmint explicitly as a climate hedge.
- The Tokaj Research Institute (Tarcal) rootstock trials — benchmarking Teleki 5C against 140 Ruggeri, 1103 Paulsen, 110 Richter on Furmint and Hárslevelű — are the single most important open experiment; results should be extended district-wide, combined with shade-netting trials and an honest PDO accommodation of dry-wine identity.

### Tolnai
- A residual district (carved from Szekszárd in 1998 to insulate Bikavér branding from Paks nuclear adjacency) whose mixed white/red identity and absence of a flagship blend is a branding weakness and, under warming, a missed adaptation lever.
- The Tűzkő Estate (Antinori since 2000, ~150–176 ha) is a *de facto* technology transfer from Tuscany and the district's most valuable climate-adaptation asset; the rest of the district is data-poor, bulk-channel-dependent and fragmented.
- Formally admit heat-tolerant Mediterranean whites (Vermentino, Fiano, Grüner Veltliner — already at Tűzkő) into the Tolnai PDO premium category, and create a Tolnai flagship cuvée designation distinct from Szekszárdi Bikavér.

### Villányi
- Hungary's southernmost and warmest district is, counter-intuitively, **not the fastest-warming** — Lakatos & Nagy (2025) show that northern districts warm faster in absolute index change, so Villány stays within Winkler V rather than jumping into a "V+" off-the-scale class (contested finding).
- The risk is not loss of suitability but **loss of stylistic identity** — the Bordeaux-analogue Cabernet Franc character is drifting toward Languedoc/southern-Rhône profile.
- The 2014 Villányi Franc rules (100 % Cabernet Franc, ≥24 months ageing, reduced yields, deliberate brake on over-extraction and over-oaking) are already a climate-resilience protocol; extend their logic to all super-premium Villány reds and trial Marselan, Petit Verdot, Tannat and Aglianico on the highest Szársomlyó parcels.

### Zalai
- Explicitly flagged by Klímapolitikai Intézet (2023) and Lakatos & Nagy (2025) as one of Hungary's two or three provisional "climate winners" — the wettest Hungarian wine district (~730–780 mm), Atlantic-influenced, and growing season mean has risen +2.77 °C since 1961–1990 with net quality improvement.
- The verdict is conditional: under RCP8.5 end-century, Zala approximates the current Szekszárd/Villány envelope, which would end its cool-climate refuge status; fungal pressure makes organic conversion structurally harder than elsewhere.
- Consolidate Pinot Noir and Grüner Veltliner as explicit flagship varieties (positioning Zala as the Hungarian default for cool-climate Pinot as Eger and Szekszárd warm out of it), and build a cross-border cool-climate alliance with Styria / Prekmurje / Međimurje.

## 4. Variety-level recommendations

For each variety: **current trajectory** (observed so far), **recommended action by district** (where growers should keep, expand, retreat or experiment), and **climate-hedge alternatives** (the next-best variety if the current one fails).

### Furmint
- **Trajectory:** Climate-robust on its home ground (Tokaj, Somló) due to thick skins, efficient stomatal control and late ripening; Austria is now planting it as a climate hedge.
- **Action:** Expand at Tokaj (dry-wine segment), at Somló (as acidity insurance for Juhfark), and plant in Badacsony, Balaton-felvidék, Balatonfüred-Csopak (already permitted in the PDO) as a climate hedge. Protect and expand at Pécsi.
- **Hedge:** Hárslevelű; Kéknyelű (very local); late-ripening Riesling clones.

### Hárslevelű
- **Trajectory:** Similar to Furmint — botrytis-tolerant, thick-skinned, late-ripening, holds acid well.
- **Action:** Expand at Tokaj alongside dry Furmint; plant as climate hedge in Somló, Balaton PDOs, Eger (permitted in Egri Csillag), Pécs.
- **Hedge:** Furmint; Olaszrizling from later clones.

### Kékfrankos (Blaufränkisch)
- **Trajectory:** Late-ripening, heat-tolerant, holds acid and colour into warm vintages; the only Hungarian red variety explicitly identified in the literature as climatically robust across the full north-to-south gradient.
- **Action:** Consolidate in Sopron (already 46 % of plantings; the "capital of Kékfrankos") as the cross-border Blaufränkisch anchor with Mittelburgenland. Expand at Mátra ("the new home of Kékfrankos" per Wines of Hungary), Bükk (the mountain-fringe freshness story), Eger (Bikavér backbone). Retain at Szekszárd and Villány.
- **Hedge:** Cabernet Franc where fuller body is acceptable; Syrah under warmer future; Kadarka for a distinct lighter red.

### Olaszrizling (Welschriesling)
- **Trajectory:** Heat-tolerant and widely adapted, but the classical "crisp, bitter-almond, mineral" style becomes increasingly difficult under warming at lakeshore and lower-elevation sites.
- **Action:** Retreat from the hottest lakeshore ramps (Balatonboglár) and red-sandstone lowlands (Balatonfüred-Csopak); consolidate on dolomite, marl and upper-slope basalt. Continue as workhorse in Zala, Eger, Bükk. Shift to later-ripening clones everywhere.
- **Hedge:** Rajnai rizling; Furmint; Grüner Veltliner.

### Kadarka
- **Trajectory:** Hungary's signature heritage red is thin-skinned, late-ripening, powdery-mildew-sensitive and the earliest Szekszárd red to leave its safe operating space under warming — yet it is also heat-tolerant on paper and could suit Csongrád and Hajós-Baja.
- **Action:** Protect the Heimann & Fiai Szekszárd clonal programme (smaller berry, looser cluster, thicker skin). Pilot in Csongrád and Hajós-Baja as an own-rooted heritage heat-tolerant variety. Retain the mandatory ≥5 % in Szekszárdi Bikavér as the variety's identity anchor. Revive in Mátra as a historic flagship.
- **Hedge:** Cabernet Franc for fuller Pannon-region style; Portugieser / Kékoportó for lighter heritage reds.

### Cserszegi fűszeres
- **Trajectory:** Heat-tolerant because of its short cycle (harvestable early to mid-August before the worst late-summer stress), aromatic but losing complexity under extreme heat. Hungary's second-most-planted white.
- **Action:** Expand in Kunság (Bianca-adjacent climate pivot), Neszmély (Hilltop's flagship export white), Hajós-Baja (everyday-drinking segment), Zala (as cool-climate alternative).
- **Hedge:** Irsai Olivér (even earlier but more aroma loss); Zenit; Zefír.

### Ezerjó
- **Trajectory:** Móri hungarikum, high acid, late ripening, historically 4th-most-planted Hungarian variety but now ~1,655 ha nationally (vs >14,000 ha in 1970). Losing its cool-climate niche in Mór under warming.
- **Action:** Start a dedicated clonal-selection programme (currently does not exist). Retain as premium category in Móri PDO. The ~1,356 ha in Kunság is mostly bulk quality — not the strategic target. Plant the Ezerjó × Pinot Gris crossing **Generosa** as a partial hedge (already in Móri Premium).
- **Hedge:** Generosa; Zeus (also Ezerjó-derived); Olaszrizling on warmer slopes.

### Cirfandli (Zierfandler)
- **Trajectory:** **Paradoxical winner** — Ipacs Szabó's quantified observation that Cirfandli produces outstanding wine in 6/10 vintages now vs 2/10 historically is the clearest producer-side evidence in Hungary of a climate-positive late-ripening variety. But district area has fallen 40 % in 5 years.
- **Action:** Pécs is the only commercial home worldwide; conserve through the PTE gene bank. Reposition toward late-harvest and off-dry styles where warming is an asset; use amphora and glass ageing, avoid new oak. Protect with the hungarikum / heritage-protection framework (§6).
- **Hedge:** Hárslevelű, Tramini (similar spice profile); Furmint.

### Juhfark
- **Trajectory:** Somló's endemic, ~80–150 ha globally, celebrated for piercing acidity. Under warming the variety is becoming physiologically mismatched to its historical home (acid loss, style drift).
- **Action:** North-aspect and upper-slope plantings on Somló-hegy, Ság-hegy and Kis-Somló as the only refuge. Start a controlled ripening-curve study of malic acid degradation at elevated temperature (the single most urgent missing study). Protect through hungarikum / heritage framework.
- **Hedge:** Furmint on Somló basalt; Olaszrizling as acid insurance.

### Kéknyelű
- **Trajectory:** Badacsony endemic, Hungarikum since 2025, ~45–47 ha of which ~45 in Badacsony. Currently benefiting from warming (the "Kéknyelű golden age" narrative), but the district-as-system is at the edge of its envelope.
- **Action:** Continue expansion under the existing PDO; plant Rózsakő (Kéknyelű × Budai zöld) as the engineered pollinator solving the female-flower bottleneck. Commission research on flowering-period heat stress under genetic bottleneck conditions (the critical gap).
- **Hedge:** Furmint on basalt; Olaszrizling; Rózsakő.

### Chardonnay
- **Trajectory:** Shifts toward riper, fuller style. Base-wine Chardonnay is structurally compromised at Etyek-Buda and on the Balatonboglár lakeshore.
- **Action:** For sparkling base, retreat to cooler Buda Hills, Velence Hills, inland Külső-Somogy, and inland Neszmély blocks; plant slow-ripening Dijon clones (95, 96) and lower-vigour rootstocks (3309C, Riparia Gloire). For still Chardonnay in Balatonboglár, Szekszárd, Tolna and Villány, accept the drift into southern Rhône white style and manage canopy for shading.
- **Hedge:** Grüner Veltliner; Pinot Blanc; Furmint as sparkling base (already at Garamvári).

### Cabernet Franc
- **Trajectory:** Clear climate winner across Pannon region; Villány is positioned as Europe's distinctive single-region Cabernet Franc terroir alongside Loire right bank and Bordeaux right bank. Hungary holds ~67 % of recent global new Cabernet Franc plantings (trade-press figure).
- **Action:** Consolidate as super-premium flagship at Villány (Villányi Franc rules); expand at Szekszárd, Tolna, Pécs, Eger (Bikavér blends). Use the 2014 Villányi Franc PDO rules (discouraging over-extraction and over-oaking) as a template for climate-era premium red style.
- **Hedge:** Merlot for earlier-ripening blend component; Syrah under warmest future scenarios.

### Pinot Noir
- **Trajectory:** Thermally pushed out of the Pannon region (Villány, Szekszárd, Eger valley) and off the Balaton south-shore loess ramp under warming. Consolidates toward the coolest western and northern districts.
- **Action:** **Retreat to the Hungarian climate refuges**: Zala (Bussay, Dóka Éva as the default), Sopron, Pannonhalma (Abbey signature red), upper Nagy-Eged at Eger, Külső-Somogy uplands at Balatonboglár, Buda Hills at Etyek-Buda.
- **Hedge:** Kékfrankos for lighter Pannon reds; Kadarka for a distinct heritage expression.

### Bianca (PIWI hybrid)
- **Trajectory:** Disease-resistant (major-gene downy mildew resistance), 2–4 sprays/season, well adapted to sandy poor soils. ~90 % of all Hungarian Bianca plantings are in Kunság.
- **Action:** **Designate Bianca as Kunság's primary climate-adaptation lever**; include explicitly in PDO rebuild discussions; support through CAP eco-schemes for low-input organic production. Plant as hedge in Hajós-Baja and Csongrád.
- **Hedge:** Generosa, Aletta, Zalagyöngye, Turán (other Hungarian resistant hybrids).

### Minor strategic varieties to watch
- **Arany sárfehér and Kövidinka** (Kunság endemics): late-ripening, high-acid, technically ideal for warming-climate sparkling base. Actively promote as Kunság's sparkling pivot.
- **Rózsakő** (Kéknyelű × Budai zöld): Badacsony-bred, solves Kéknyelű's pollination bottleneck, climate-aligned.
- **Generosa, Zeus, Zéta** (Hungarian crossings involving Ezerjó/Furmint parentage): high acid retention, deserve expanded trials.
- **Grüner Veltliner (Zöld Veltelíni)**: Gains suitability across western districts (Etyek, Neszmély, Sopron, Zala, Pannonhalma) and already planted at Tűzkő Tolna.
- **Syrah, Tempranillo, Grenache, Tannat, Touriga Nacional, Aglianico, Viognier, Assyrtiko, Fiano, Vermentino**: Tier 3 experimental pivot for Csongrád, Kunság, Hajós-Baja, Villány, Szekszárd and Tolna. Not yet validated for Hungarian radiation/photoperiod.

## 5. Adaptation toolkit

### Tier 1 — proven, deploy now

Evidence status: cited across multiple district files with direct Hungarian field evidence or unambiguous international consensus.

- **Rootstock substitution.** Shift from Teleki 5C, SO4 and Kober 5BB to drought-tolerant **140 Ruggeri, 1103 Paulsen, 110 Richter** on all new plantings. Tokaj Research Institute (Tarcal) trials on Furmint and Hárslevelű are the definitive Hungarian reference. Applicable to every district, highest priority in Kunság, Csongrád, Hajós-Baja, Villány, Szekszárd, Mátra, Pécs, Móri.
- **Canopy management for shading.** Higher trunk / fruiting-wire training (from classical ~70 cm toward 100–110 cm), delayed and reduced leaf removal (retain canopy on afternoon side), double-sided leaf removal only on the morning side. Standard warm-climate toolkit now being applied across all 22 districts.
- **Late pruning to delay budburst.** Pernesz et al. (Eger) Kékfrankos late-pruning trial and Misik et al. (Eger) post-véraison apical defoliation are the published Hungarian field evidence; documented 3–10 day phenological delay with preserved TA (Vercesi et al. 2023 Chardonnay). Directly transferable to all frost-prone northern and upper-slope sites. Caveat: Misik et al. report yield trade-off — the long-term economic balance is unproven.
- **Altitude and aspect migration where headroom exists.** **Mátra** has the clearest case (Sár-hegy to ~500 m), followed by **Eger** (Nagy-Eged to ~400 m), **Villány** (Szársomlyó 350–440 m), **Pécs** (Mecsek footslope 300–440 m), **Bükk** (200–300 m rhyolite tuff terraces). Districts with no headroom: Kunság, Csongrád (both flat), Nagy-Somló (tiny footprint), Móri (max ~300 m).
- **Harvest timing by lab markers, not calendar.** Already standard among premium producers district-wide. Compressed harvest windows (now ≤6 weeks) force capacity investment; smaller producers struggle. Night harvesting standard in premium Balaton, Villány and Neszmély operations.
- **Cover cropping and mulching.** Universal recommendation for soil-moisture retention, erosion control, soil carbon. Adoption is uneven — loess-ramp districts (Balatonboglár, Hajós-Baja, Tolna, Szekszárd, Pannonhalma) have specific published support (Bertalan-Balázs et al. 2022 for Neszmély erosion). Most effective where convective-storm intensification is driving runoff losses.
- **Restoration of historic dry-stone terraces.** Balaton basalt buttes (Badacsony, Balaton-felvidék, Nagy-Somló) and loess-ramp bluffs (Tolna Györköny) — simultaneously cultural heritage and climate-resilience investment.

### Tier 2 — in trial, watch closely

Evidence status: emerging Hungarian field trials or strong analogues from neighbouring countries, but not yet validated at district scale.

- **Bianca and other resistant (PIWI) varieties.** Bianca is already Kunság's dominant climate-adaptation lever (~90 % of Hungarian plantings). Souvignier Gris, Cabernet Blanc, Muscaris, Solaris, Bronner are trialled at Balatonfüred-Csopak (organic Kódex context), Sopron (biodynamic Weninger), Zala (fungal-pressure necessity). Bianca's red counterpart Turán and the Hungarian crossings Generosa, Aletta, Zalagyöngye deserve expanded district-level trials.
- **Shading nets and kaolin / calcium-silicate sunscreens.** Tokaj Research Institute plastic shade netting during berry maturation shows preliminary positive results. Experimental on premium Kéknyelű parcels in Badacsony; being discussed at Somló, Szekszárd, Villány, Pécs. Full economic evaluation still missing.
- **Precision viticulture and vineyard sensors.** Syngenta's Mátra disease-forecasting decision-support tool is operational. No district-wide soil-moisture / vineyard ET monitoring network in any of the 22 districts. Highest return in Móri (to close the Lakatos & Nagy water-deficit gap) and in Kunság (to make the groundwater problem visible at plot level).
- **PDO rule reform as climate protocol.** The Csopak Kódex (2013, Balatonfüred-Csopaki) and the Villányi Franc rules (2014, Villányi) are working precedents — restricting cultivar, capping yields, capping alcohol, requiring high-density mature plantings. Both function as climate-resilience protocols. Use them as templates for all 22 PDO specifications.
- **Partial dealcoholisation (reverse osmosis).** Available at Tűzkő Tolna, Szekszárd larger estates and Villány. Legal status in PDO wines is a "grey area" according to the district files. Needs formal PDO clarification before scaling.

### Tier 3 — experimental or contested

Evidence status: plausible on physical grounds, but Hungarian validation is thin, contested, or blocked by policy.

- **Variety substitution toward Mediterranean cultivars.** Syrah, Tempranillo, Grenache, Marselan, Petit Verdot, Tannat, Touriga Nacional, Aglianico, Sangiovese (reds); Assyrtiko, Fiano, Vermentino, Viognier, Verdejo (whites). Lakatos & Nagy (2025) explicitly name Csongrád as a district where Grenache, Syrah and Tempranillo become cultivable — but aroma and flavour development under Hungarian radiation/photoperiod is unvalidated, and PDO rules block fast deployment. Villány, Szekszárd, Tolna and Pécs trial plots exist. **Austrian Furmint** (variety substitution in *reverse* — Austria is planting Tokaj's flagship as a climate hedge) is a live example.
- **Irrigation.** Legally restricted in Hungary. Unanimous call from the literature that this must change. Complicated by (a) the Lake Balaton water-budget for the four Balaton PDOs and Zala, (b) the Homokhátság groundwater commons collapse for Kunság, Csongrád and Hajós-Baja, and (c) PDO rules forbidding irrigation in several premium categories. **Deficit-irrigation** (regulated deficit; Kovács et al. 2023 Eger dryness-index ≥ −10 target) is the defensible compromise. Tokaj, Villány, Szekszárd, Eger and Móri discussions are active; none implemented at scale.
- **Amphora and large neutral oak vs new barrique for high-alcohol wines.** Pécs producers (Cirfandli), Balatonfüred-Csopak, Badacsony, Szekszárd — cellar-side adaptation to preserve freshness perception without stripping texture. No controlled trials.
- **Harvest-window labour pooling.** The compressed harvest window (≤6 weeks) forces simultaneous picking of previously staggered varieties (documented explicitly in Mátra 2024 grower testimony). Social/labour adaptation is identified by the Sopron file as "an open research area" with no cooperative structures yet in place.
- **PDO border modification and internal land-use re-zoning.** Lakatos & Nagy (2025) explicitly recommend this for all 22 Hungarian PDOs — politically the most sensitive and scientifically the best supported single option. Balatonfüred-Csopak could formally promote upper-slope dolomite/marl; Balatonboglár could migrate premium sparkling base inland to Külső-Somogy; Csongrád could contract back to the Tisza-alluvial core; Móri could shrink to the highest and coolest sub-zones.
- **Cross-border district alliances.** Sopron + Mittelburgenland (Austria) for Blaufränkisch; Zala + Prekmurje (Slovenia) + Međimurje (Croatia) for cool-climate whites; Tokaj + eastern Slovakia for Furmint-territory coordination. Currently absent from policy.

## 6. Policy recommendations

The eight recommendations below are derived directly from the 22 district files. They are listed in descending priority order. Every one of them is either (i) explicitly called for in the source files, (ii) supported by unanimous or near-unanimous literature convergence across districts, or (iii) the removal of an administrative blocker that is currently preventing field-level adaptation.

### Priority 1 — Ease the legal restrictions on vineyard irrigation

**Called for by:** Tokaji, Egri, Kunsági, Csongrádi, Hajós-Bajai, Villányi, Szekszárdi, Móri and Balaton district files. Unanimous across the relevant districts.

**The asks.**
- Amend Hungarian water-rights law to permit **regulated deficit irrigation** in commercial vineyards where drought conditions breach SPEI/SPI thresholds.
- Define a **nationwide dryness-index threshold** (Kovács et al. Eger 2023 dryness index ≥ −10 is the proposed reference).
- Clarify PDO compatibility: irrigation for vine survival and regulated deficit should not automatically exclude wines from PDO / Super Premium categories.
- Explicitly exclude extraction from Lake Balaton and from Homokhátság shallow groundwater; mandate alternative sources (Danube, Tisza, treated thermal-spa wastewater — the 2025 Hungarian experiment explicitly recommended for Csongrád / Kunság).
- Require **district-level water budgets** as a precondition for irrigation authorisation.

**What this unlocks:** The only credible on-farm buffer for the three Duna-region districts (Csongrád, Kunság, Hajós-Baja) and one of the two most-requested interventions in Tokaj, Eger, Villány and Szekszárd.

### Priority 2 — Modify PDO borders and internal land-use structure

**Called for by:** Lakatos & Nagy (2025) **explicit recommendation**, reinforced by Kovács et al. 2025 recommendation, reinforced by the Szekszárd Pieczka et al. 2021 vulnerability paper, and by all four Balaton PDO files.

**The asks.**
- Commission a district-by-district PDO boundary and variety-list review using the published FORESEE 4.0 / Lakatos & Nagy 2025 data. Treat it as a structured, scientific exercise — not a one-off crisis response.
- Promote upper-slope dolomite, marl and rendzina sites into **formal premium tiers** in Balatonfüred-Csopak, Balaton-felvidék, Badacsony, Somló and Eger.
- Permit **heat-tolerant Mediterranean whites and reds** (Vermentino, Fiano, Grüner Veltliner, Viognier; Syrah, Tempranillo, Touriga Nacional, Marselan, Aglianico) into premium category specifications at Villány, Szekszárd, Tolna, Pécs, Csongrád and Kunság.
- Admit **Pinot Meunier** (and optionally Riesling, Auxerrois) to the Etyeki Pezsgő PDO variety list — the single most-named grower ask in the district file.
- Explicitly allow **controlled retreat** — formal PDO re-delimitation of the most marginal Homokhátság (Kunsági, Csongrádi) parcels, backed by compensation or CAP payments.
- Use the **Csopak Kódex** and **Villányi Franc 2014** rules as templates for a new generation of climate-explicit private quality codes.

### Priority 3 — Fund extraction and publication of FORESEE 4.0 / Lakatos & Nagy 2025 supplementary tables at per-PDO resolution

**Called for by:** 19 of the 22 district files, as the highest-priority single missing data product.

**The ask.** Direct funding (HNT or MATE) to extract and publish AGST, GDD-Winkler, Huglin Index and BEDD values for each of the 22 borvidék as decadal tables from 1971 to 2100 under RCP4.5 and RCP8.5 from the **FORESEE 4.0 / 4.2 bias-corrected EURO-CORDEX ensemble** hosted at `nimbus.elte.hu/FORESEE/`. Release as open data under CC-BY with a permanent DOI.

**What this unlocks:** Every downstream planning, insurance, zoning, PDO-reform, investment and risk-management decision in Hungarian viticulture currently rests on a paper whose per-district numbers are not public. This is the cheapest, fastest single policy win available.

### Priority 4 — Establish district-level phenology and pest-monitoring networks

**Called for by:** 21 of the 22 district files (Tokaj is the only district with a partial multi-decadal phenology series).

**The asks.**
- **Dedicated, well-instrumented OMSZ/HungaroMet reference stations** inside each borvidék (Somló-hegy, Móri-árok, Badacsony mid-slope, Csopak, Villány south-front, Nagy-Eged, Bükk Bogács-tuff, Mátra Sár-hegy, Bussay Zala, Hilltop Neszmély, Pannonhalma hilltop, Tűzkő Tolna, etc.). ~22 new stations is a modest cost and transformational data improvement.
- **Multi-decadal phenology observations** (budburst → flowering → véraison → harvest) for each district's flagship varieties, submitted to the international PEP725 database. Leverage existing estate records (Abbey Pannonhalma, Tokaj Tarcal, Hilltop Neszmély, Tűzkő, Heimann) under a standardised protocol.
- **District-level pest surveillance** for *Scaphoideus titanus* (Flavescence dorée vector), *Halyomorpha halys*, *Drosophila suzukii*, *Lobesia botrana*, plus esca / GTD and the climate-disease couplings of downy and powdery mildew.
- **Aggregate at HNT / MATE level** and publish as an open annual "Hungarian viticulture climate report".

### Priority 5 — Coupled lake-vineyard mesoclimate downscaling for the four Balaton PDOs and Fertő-tó

**Called for by:** Badacsony, Balaton-felvidék, Balatonfüred-Csopak, Balatonboglár and Sopron files — all five identify this as the single most distinctive terroir factor that is not represented in any existing climate model.

**The ask.** Commission a bespoke high-resolution (sub-km) coupled lake-atmosphere-vineyard model that represents Lake Balaton's ~594 km² thermal buffer, its projected water-budget trajectory (Zala-basin runoff ≥40 % decline by 2071–2100), its albedo, evaporative cooling and radiative reflection onto the south-facing Badacsony basalt cones and the north-shore amphitheatres. Independent parallel exercise for Lake Fertő. EURO-CORDEX 12 km cannot resolve the lake.

**What this unlocks:** The only physical basis for deciding whether Badacsony, Balaton-felvidék, Balatonfüred-Csopak and Balatonboglár can survive as premium PDOs if lake area shrinks 10–20 % by 2080. And the physical basis for the entire Balaton wine-tourism economy.

### Priority 6 — Climate-explicit revision of PDO yield caps and chemistry limits

**Called for by:** Balatonfüred-Csopaki (Csopak Kódex precedent), Villányi (Villányi Franc precedent), Eger (Bikavér tiering), Szekszárd (Bikavér Premium rules), Tokaj (dry-wine accommodation), Pécs (Cirfandli style).

**The asks.**
- Lower permitted yields in premium and Super Premium categories where warming has pushed baseline ripeness upward.
- Raise permitted total acidity minima and tighten pH maxima for classical cool-style categories.
- Permit tartaric acidification within clearly defined bounds in hot-vintage declarations.
- Formalise the legal status of **partial dealcoholisation** (reverse osmosis, spinning cone) in PDO wines — currently a grey area.
- Use the Csopak Kódex and Villányi Franc 2014 rules as templates.

### Priority 7 — Cross-border Kékfrankos / Blaufränkisch climate strategy with Austria

**Called for by:** Soproni file explicitly (Felső-Pannon / Mittelburgenland is "one of the world's most coherent single-variety red-wine cultural regions"). Reinforced by Bükk, Eger, Szekszárd, Villány and Mátra as the national Kékfrankos strategy.

**The ask.** A formal bilateral Hungarian-Austrian coordination (HNT + Österreichischer Weinbauverband + Mittelburgenland DAC governance) on:
- Shared clonal resources, field trials and climate-resilience breeding.
- Common cross-border marketing ("The Blaufränkisch Belt" as a climate-refuge identity for continental cool-red wines, alongside Styria, Mosel, Loire, English sparkling).
- Coordinated PDO/DAC harmonisation.
- A shared climate-and-phenology monitoring network across the border (Sopron, Neusiedlersee-Hügelland, Mittelburgenland, Leithaberg).
- Flood/frost/hail mutual-aid protocols.

### Priority 8 — Hungarikum-status protection for the endangered indigenous varieties

**Called for by:** Badacsonyi (Kéknyelű declared Hungarikum in 2025 — a successful precedent), Móri (Ezerjó hungarikum-listed), Somlói (Juhfark), Pécsi (Cirfandli), Szekszárdi (Kadarka).

**The ask.** Extend the Hungarikum / National Value framework to all five critically endangered indigenous varieties — **Kéknyelű, Ezerjó, Juhfark, Cirfandli, Kadarka** — with:
- A **national clonal selection and breeding programme** for each (none currently exist for Ezerjó, Cirfandli, Juhfark, or Kéknyelű; Szekszárd's Heimann Kadarka programme is the domestic template).
- **Gene bank back-up** at the PTE Pécs Research Institute (already world's 6th-largest vine gene bank).
- **Dedicated research funding** — the highest-value single line item is the Somló Juhfark malic-acid ripening-curve study, at an order-of-magnitude cost of ~€150k for three vintages.
- **Heritage-marketing protection** against dilution (e.g. the "Móri Aszú" name being legally unavailable because Aszú is reserved to Tokaj is a precedent that could be leveraged productively for the other four).
- **Producer-side price-premium support** through CAP eco-schemes to keep the varieties economically viable during the transition.

## 7. Funding and EU CAP alignment

All eight policy recommendations map cleanly onto existing EU funding instruments. The Hungarian wine sector has been under-using these relative to France, Italy, Spain and Austria.

### CAP Strategic Plan 2023–2027 — Hungarian Rural Development Programme (Vidékfejlesztési Program, VP)
- **Eco-schemes:** Cover cropping, reduced tillage, organic conversion, PIWI-variety plantings, biodiversity buffers, erosion control on loess slopes — all directly align with the Tier 1 and Tier 2 adaptation toolkit above. Bussay (Zala), Kreinbacher (Somló), Weninger (Sopron), Fekete (Somló) are existing models. Expand uptake to the 22 borvidék, with a specific Bianca/PIWI eco-scheme for Kunság and Csongrád.
- **Agro-environmental-climatic measures (AECM):** Terraces restoration, anti-erosion works on the Balaton basalt buttes, Györköny loess press-house bluffs and Tolna ridges, dry-stone wall rehabilitation, traditional vineyard-landscape preservation.
- **Investment support:** Drought-tolerant rootstock replanting, hail nets on premium blocks, frost-protection infrastructure (wind machines, candles, aspersion), drip irrigation infrastructure (contingent on Priority 1 legal reform), cold-chain and night-harvest equipment, cellar refrigeration.
- **Knowledge exchange and innovation (AKIS):** District-level phenology and pest-monitoring networks (Priority 4); producer training on late pruning, canopy management, acidification, PIWI varieties.

### Horizon Europe — climate adaptation and biodiversity calls
- **Cluster 6 (Food, Bioeconomy, Natural Resources, Agriculture and Environment):** The five priority research gaps — coupled lake-vineyard mesoclimate model (Priority 5), endangered-variety malic-acid physiology (Priority 8), district-level phenology networks (Priority 4), per-PDO FORESEE downscaling (Priority 3), climate-disease coupled modelling for DM/PM/esca — are all directly eligible.
- **Horizon Europe Mission "Adaptation to Climate Change":** Hungary has been a low-uptake Member State. The 22 borvidék are a coherent, high-profile candidate site for a Mission-level demonstration.
- **Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions:** Individual Fellowships for district-level wine-climate PhD and postdoctoral work, hosted at MATE, PTE, Eszterházy Károly Catholic University, and the Tokaj Research Institute.

### LIFE Programme
- **Climate Change Adaptation sub-programme:** Landscape-scale erosion control on loess (Neszmély, Tolna, Pannonhalma, Szekszárd), basalt and dolomite restoration (Balaton witness-hills), Homokhátság re-wetting and wetland restoration (Kunság, Csongrád, Hajós-Baja).
- **Nature and Biodiversity sub-programme:** Vineyard biodiversity corridors, Natura 2000 compatibility in Mátrai Tájvédelmi Körzet upslope plantings, integrated vine-forest landscape management in the Mecsek and Zemplén.

### EU Wine CMO (Common Market Organisation) measures
- **Restructuring and conversion of vineyards:** The primary EU instrument for variety substitution and replanting. Explicit alignment with Priority 2 (PDO reform) and the Tier 1/Tier 3 variety recommendations. Csongrád and Kunság replants to Bianca, Mediterranean varieties, or controlled retreat; Mátra upslope migration; Etyek-Buda Pinot Meunier and cooler-clone Chardonnay; Villány, Szekszárd and Tolna Mediterranean-reds trials.
- **Promotion on third-country markets:** Support the "Hungarian climate refuge" (Sopron, Zala, Pannonhalma) and "Blaufränkisch Belt" export positioning.
- **Green harvest and harvest insurance:** Buffer extreme-vintage risk at Csongrád, Kunság and the 2024-hit districts.

### Hungarian national instruments
- **Nemzeti Agrárgazdasági Kamara (NAK) / HNT / Ministry of Agriculture:** The domestic channel for PDO reform (Priority 2, 6) and for the cross-border Austrian coordination (Priority 7).
- **Hungarikum framework:** Already active for Kéknyelű (2025) and Ezerjó; extend to Juhfark, Cirfandli, Kadarka (Priority 8).
- **Climate- and environmental-fund instruments:** Align with the European Parliament Think Tank 2025 briefing on Hungary's climate change strategy.

## 8. What growers can do this season, this decade, and this generation

| Timescale | White districts (Balaton, Felső-Pannon, Somló, Mór, Eger/Bükk whites, Tokaj, Pécs) | Red districts (Villány, Szekszárd, Tolna, Eger reds, Mátra, Bükk reds, Sopron) | Alföld districts (Kunság, Csongrád, Hajós-Baja) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **This season** | Late pruning to delay budburst; raise fruiting wire to 100–110 cm; retain east-side canopy for shade; start harvesting by lab markers not calendar; night harvest for aromatics; acidify within PDO limits. | Shift leaf-removal to morning side only; higher trellises; cover crops between rows; earlier picks for cool-style wines; retain whole-cluster inclusion; reduce new-oak share. | Cover crops to bind sand; straw or pruning-chip mulching; kaolin sunburn spray on most exposed blocks; earlier picks for Cserszegi fűszeres, Irsai Olivér; defer replanting decisions to the best chernozem-sand mosaics. |

Accept PDO style drift and rewrite the rules to reflect it. Formally promote upper-slope and north-facing parcels to premium tier. | Rewrite district identity around a climate-era premium blend code (Bikavér 2.0, Villányi Franc extended logic). Embed Mediterranean reds into formal PDO categories. Treat cool-climate refuges (Nagy-Eged, upper Szársomlyó, Bükk upper terraces, Sopron schist) as strategic cultural heritage. Build the cross-border Blaufränkisch Belt. | **Structural contraction.** Consolidate planted area on the best soils with the best water-retention; retreat from the driest Homokhátság cores; reposition the PDO around heat-tolerant and resistant varieties (Bianca, Cserszegi, Kadarka own-rooted, Mediterranean reds); align tourism with the Hajós Pincefalu heritage asset and the Csongrád-Szeged-border cross-regional circuit; accept that total district area will be smaller and be honest about it. |

### Five highest-leverage grower actions, by season

1. **Spring (budburst → flowering):** Late pruning to delay budburst by 3–10 days, re-coupling phenology to the frost-free window. Universal, cheap, published Hungarian field evidence from Eger (Pernesz et al.).
2. **Early summer (flowering → véraison):** Retain canopy cover on the afternoon side; delay leaf removal; raise fruiting wire above the soil heat layer. Universal, published in Badacsony (Werner & Kozma et al.) and applicable district-wide.
3. **Summer (véraison → pre-harvest):** Switch from calendar-based to lab-marker-based harvest decisions (sugar, acidity, pH, aroma precursors). Night harvesting in premium operations. Multi-pass picking where the compressed window and simultaneous-ripening problem (documented for 2024 in Mátra and Tolna) forces it.
4. **Harvest (August–October):** Rapid chilling, whole-bunch pressing for whites, cooler fermentation temperatures, partial whole-cluster inclusion for reds, earlier bottling for the aromatic categories. Tartaric acidification within the PDO and legal bounds for hot vintages.
5. **Winter (dormant → bud):** Cover crops between rows, mulching, organic-matter building. Restoration of historic dry-stone terraces on basalt buttes and loess bluffs. Replant plans for the next spring using drought-tolerant rootstocks (140 Ru, 1103 P, 110 R) and the district-specific variety mix the grower has chosen.
