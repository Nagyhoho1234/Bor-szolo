# Climate Change Impacts on Wine Quality in the Pécsi Wine District (Pannon Region, Hungary)

*An exhaustive, citation-backed synthesis*
*Compiled 2026-04-07*

## 1. District Profile

The **Pécsi borvidék** (Pécs Wine District) is one of the four PDO wine districts forming Hungary's **Pannon wine region** (together with Szekszárd, Tolna and Villány) [Wiki-Pannon]. It occupies an ~80 km east–west strip in Baranya County along the southern and south-eastern slopes of the **Mecsek** (a limestone/Permian-sandstone hill range rising above 600 m), the rolling hills around Szederkény and Mohács, and the south-eastern foot of the Zselic Hills [TasteHungary-Pécs; WinesOfHungary-Pécs; bor.hu-EN].

**Area under vine.** Sources report different figures depending on year and definition:
- **~673 ha** (Taste Hungary, PDO area) [TasteHungary-Pécs]
- **~720 ha** (PDO) / **~640 ha active**, white >480 ha, red ~158 ha [WineTourism-Pécs; TasteHungary-Pécs]
- **~823 ha** (Hungarian Wikipedia, older figure) [Wiki-HU-Pécs]
- Of ~6,998 ha of first-class suitable slope, only ~700 ha are currently under vine, pointing to a long-term contraction from historical highs [TasteHungary-Pécs].

So the working order of magnitude is **~600–700 ha** — a small district, roughly 1% of Hungary's vineyard area (national total fell from 69,000 ha in 2018 to ~61,000 ha by 2023) [TasteHungary-Stats].

**PDO.** The district is protected under the **"Pécs" oltalom alatt álló eredetmegjelölés (OEM/PDO)**, registered with the Hungarian Ministry of Agriculture [kormany.hu-Pécs-OEM]. A 2012 initial product specification was amended in 2016 to add siller, sparkling and semi-sparkling wines in all colours [bor.hu-EN]. Two sub-zones are recognised: **Pécsi körzet** (Boda, Cserkút, Hosszúhetény, Olasz, Szajk, Szederkény, Versend) and **Szigetvári körzet** (Helesfa, Botykapeterd, Mozsgó, Nagypeterd, Nyugotszenterzsébet, Szentlőrinc, Szigetvár) [Wiki-HU-Pécs].

**Flagship varieties** — whites dominate (>75% of area):
- **Cirfandli** — *the* district's signature. Technically imported from Austria (where it is Zierfandler, Thermenregion) in the early 19th century by the Provost of Pécs, but Pécs is today its only commercial home in Hungary and essentially its only home outside a small corner of Lower Austria [TasteHungary-Pécs; Wiki-Zierfandler]. Locally treated as an indigenous heritage variety and legally tied to the "Pécsi Cirfandli" name. Research-vineyard inventories show a dramatic contraction: **down 40% over five years to just 13.9 ha by 2024** [BorEsPiac-2025].
- **Olaszrizling** (Welschriesling) — post-phylloxera leading variety, backbone of everyday whites [Wiki-HU-Pécs].
- **Hárslevelű**, **Chardonnay**, **Tramini** (Gewürztraminer), **Rajnai Rizling** (Riesling), **Furmint**, **Zöld Veltelini** (Grüner Veltliner), **Királyleányka**, **Müller-Thurgau**, **Ottonel Muskotály**, **Zengő**, **Zenit** [Wiki-HU-Pécs; TasteHungary-Pécs].
- Reds (historically secondary, increasingly important under warming): **Kékfrankos** (Blaufränkisch), **Pinot Noir**, **Cabernet Sauvignon**, **Cabernet Franc**, **Merlot**, and heritage **Kadarka** (introduced by Balkan settlers fleeing the Ottomans) [TasteHungary-Pécs].

**Key institutions.** The **University of Pécs Wine Estate (PTE Szőlészeti és Borászati Kutatóintézet)** hosts one of Hungary's foremost viticulture/oenology research institutes and the **world's sixth-largest vine gene bank (~1,500 accessions)**, including rare Hungarian heirlooms — a critically important adaptation asset [TasteHungary-Pécs]. Notable private producers include **Lisicza**, **Matias**, and **Wekler** [TasteHungary-Pécs].

**Wine tourism.** The **Pécs-Mecseki Borút** wine route and the UNESCO-listed city of Pécs anchor one of Hungary's best-developed enotourism clusters [VisitHungary-Pécs; bor.hu-EN].

## 2. Terroir & Soils

**Aspect and topography.** South- and south-east-facing slopes of the Mecsek, elevations mainly **110–300 m** with experimental and marginal plots up to **380–440 m** (Hosszúhetény, Pécsvárad) [TasteHungary-Pécs; Wiki-HU-Pécs; bor.hu-EN]. The 200–300 m relative relief of the Mecsek rising from the north produces:
1. A **wind shadow** blocking cold northern air masses,
2. **Warm downslope (foehn-like) mountain breezes** from the forested ridge that raise night minima and **mitigate spring frost** [bor.hu-HU].

**Geology and soils.** Highly heterogeneous:
- **Permian red sandstone** and **Triassic limestone/calcareous rock** on the Mecsek itself
- **Acidic, iron-rich brown forest soils** over red sandstone
- **Rendzinas** over limestone and marl
- **Loess** and **adobe (clayey loess)** on the southern footslopes
- **Pannonian Sea sediments** and **clay shale** patches
- **Weathered sandstone** parent material
- Around Szigetvár/Versend sub-zones loess dominates [TasteHungary-Pécs; bor.hu-EN].

The mosaic of limestone-derived calcareous soils (favouring structured whites — Cirfandli, Olaszrizling, Chardonnay) and iron-rich acidic browns (favouring reds) explains the district's broad varietal portfolio.

**Mediterranean character.** The combination of south aspect, sheltered position, deep loess reservoirs for summer water storage, and sunshine-rich exposure places Pécs among Hungary's most Mediterranean-influenced terroirs, comparable only to neighbouring Villány [TasteHungary-Pécs].

**Sub-Mediterranean signature.** Baranya County's southern and south-eastern areas "belong to Hungary's warmest regions due to favourable radiation conditions" [bor.hu-HU]. Pécs is classically described as **the warmest Hungarian wine district outside Villány** [Wiki-HU-Pécs; bor.hu-EN]. Within a Huglin Index framework this places baseline Pécs near the **"Temperate warm" (HI+1, 2100–2400)** class in the late-20th-century climatology — conditions classically suitable for late-ripening whites (Cirfandli, Riesling, Furmint) and mid-season reds (Kékfrankos, Merlot, Cabernet Franc) [general Huglin class mapping; Lakatos & Nagy 2025].

## 4. Observed Trends [OBSERVED]

1. **Warming, accelerating since ~1980.** "The warming rate increased significantly in the last four decades, particularly for the summer, affecting the **Mecsek**, the central Danube region and the eastern part of the country the most" [WB-CCKP-Hungary]. This places the Pécsi district in one of Hungary's three fastest-warming sub-regions.
2. **Growing season temperature.** Lakatos & Nagy (2025) document statistically significant positive trends in **AGST, GDD (Winkler), Huglin Index, and BEDD** across all 22 Hungarian wine regions 1971–2020, including Pécs. National range of observed AGST rise is consistent with ~1.0–1.5 °C over the last 50 years [Lakatos & Nagy 2025]. **[GAP]** The published text does not tabulate Pécs-specific baseline values — see §14.
3. **Huglin class shift.** Under the observed warming, Pannon-region HI has already climbed toward the **"Warm" (HI+2, 2400–3000)** class, approaching thresholds where Mediterranean varieties (Grenache, Syrah, Carignan) become climatically viable [Lakatos & Nagy 2025; MDPI-Szekszárd 2021].
4. **Precipitation becoming extreme.** Pécs-based hydrometeorological network analysis (1971–2020) documented **increasing frequencies of extreme precipitation events (EPE)** at daily thresholds of ≥20, ≥30 and ≥40 mm, with OMSZ gridded products *underestimating* the true EPE frequency relative to a denser 10-station PHN array (2013–2020) [HunGeoBull-EPE-SW-Hungary]. Summer rainfall "has become less frequent but more intense" nationally, with stronger flash-flood potential in hilly districts like Pécs [WB-CCKP-Hungary].
5. **Seasonal shift.** Spring and autumn precipitation has declined; western Transdanubia has lost up to **15%** of mid-20th-century annual totals in some areas [WB-CCKP-Hungary].
6. **Recent extreme years.**
   - **2022** — Hungary's driest and hottest year on record; nationwide compound drought-heatwave; "hottest summer since 1901"; eastern Hungary crop losses near-total; a 7-week near-zero-rain period from mid-June devastated agriculture [MDPI-Forests-2022; Pinke et al. 2024].
   - **2024** — "quite similar" to 2022 with severely dry summer and major agricultural impacts [Euronews-2024; Copernicus-2024].
   - 2018 — early harvests (starting in early August) reported by Hungarian producers to avoid over-ripening [Organic-Wineries-Hungary].
7. **Producer perception.** Hungarian organic winemakers report having "perceived the effects of climate change for about 10–15 years", with weather becoming markedly less predictable [Organic-Wineries-Hungary].
8. **Paradoxical Cirfandli quality improvement.** István Ipacs Szabó (head winemaker, PTE Pécs Research Institute) quantified the shift: **"Classical teaching was that Cirfandli gave outstanding wine in only ~2 vintages out of 10; today, 6 out of 10 vintages produce outstanding Cirfandli."** [BorEsPiac-2025]. This is one of the clearest producer-side quantifications of warming benefits for a late-ripening indigenous Hungarian cultivar anywhere in the literature.

## 5. Phenology & Yield [OBSERVED + PROJECTED]

**Observed.** Hungarian phenological modelling has focused on Chardonnay, Szürkebarát (Pinot gris), Pinot blanc, Riesling and Hárslevelű — all represented in Pécs — with budburst and bloom timing now modelable from heat-sum indices that require recalibration every decade due to the moving baseline [Szenteleki et al., Lakatos et al. 2015]. Across Central Europe a ~180 km northward shift of suitability borders per 1 °C is the standard rule of thumb [Hannah et al. 2013], implying Pécs' present climate will migrate to northern Slovakia / southern Poland analog within decades.

**Specific to Pécs.** [GAP] No published Pécs-specific phenological time series has been located. However, the producer observation that Cirfandli now ripens successfully 6/10 vintages vs. the historic 2/10 [BorEsPiac-2025] is a de facto phenological indicator — Cirfandli historically ripened *later than Rotgipfler* [Wiki-Zierfandler], and was at the cool edge of its thermal niche in Pécs.

**Yield.** Post-phylloxera and Habsburg-era contraction has left only ~10% of 1st-class suitable slope under vine [TasteHungary-Pécs], so yield responses to climate are confounded with structural contraction. No district-level yield time series has been located in the open literature [GAP].

**[PROJECTED]** Budburst advance of 1–2 weeks by mid-century, harvest advance of 2–4 weeks by end-of-century for mid-ripening varieties in the Pannon region under RCP8.5 — consistent with European-wide projections [Fraga et al., MDPI Atmosphere 2021].

## 6. Grape Chemistry — the Cirfandli Trajectory

**Cirfandli (Zierfandler)** is physiologically defined by:
- **Very late ripening** (later than Rotgipfler) [Wiki-Zierfandler]
- **High natural acidity retention** through ripening [WorldsBestWines-Zierfandler]
- **Susceptibility to noble rot (Botrytis cinerea)** — the basis of Pécsi Cirfandli's traditional late-harvest and aszú-style expressions [Wiki-Zierfandler; Wiki-HU-Pécs]
- **Alcohol risk in warm vintages** — "in warmer vintages, however, high alcohol levels can be a problem" [Gebeshuber/Thermenregion analog]

**Observed trajectory in Pécs under warming** [OBSERVED, BorEsPiac-2025; PécsiBorozó]:
- **Alcohol escalation.** Commercial dry Pécsi Cirfandli routinely reaches **14.5% vol** (e.g., Lisicza dry) — historically a rarity for this cool-climate late-ripener.
- **Acid decline.** Expert tasting panels flag that "dry versions lack sufficient acidity to provide liveliness, while sweet versions lose vibrancy" [PécsiBorozó]. Balance between residual sugar and natural acidity is now a primary winemaking problem.
- **Sugar accumulation is reliable.** Noble-rot botrytisation is reported to be increasingly accessible — 2023 late-harvest samples scored well with modest residual sugar (~7.5 g/L) and "orange peel, dried ginger" aromatics [BorEsPiac-2025].
- **Heat damage to fruit structure.** Ipacs Szabó explicitly notes: **"extreme heat damages fruit structure more severely [in Cirfandli] than in international varieties"** [BorEsPiac-2025]. This is the key caveat to the "6/10 vintages now outstanding" statement — peak heatwaves still create off-vintages.
- **Vintage dispersion widens.** 2022 dry samples struggled with premature oxidation and "age fatigue"; 2023 late-harvest worked far better [BorEsPiac-2025].

For the other whites (Olaszrizling, Chardonnay, Tramini, Hárslevelű), the same general pattern documented regionally applies: **+0.5–1.5% vol potential alcohol, −1–2 g/L total acidity (TA), +0.1–0.3 pH unit** over the last two to three decades [MDPI-Szekszárd 2021; general Pannon]. For reds (Kékfrankos, Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot) the pattern is more favourable: **more consistent phenolic ripening, lower green-pyrazine character, deeper colour** — though with risk of over-ripe, raisined character in extreme years [TasteHungary-Pécs].

## 7. Sensory & Style

**Cirfandli identity at risk.** The key sensory tension in the district is the evolving style of Pécsi Cirfandli:
- *Traditional style:* Delicately floral nose, spicy palate, moderate alcohol, noticeable acidity, often off-dry or late-harvest, Botrytis-kissed in top years [TasteHungary-Pécs; Wiki-HU-Pécs].
- *Emerging style under warming:* Fuller-bodied, higher alcohol (14–14.5%+), lower perceived freshness, more honeyed/tropical, more oxidative aging risk, greater vintage divergence between dry and sweet expressions [PécsiBorozó; BorEsPiac-2025].
- *Producer uncertainty* on style direction is explicit: "winemakers lack consensus on optimal production methods: whether the variety is necessary, whether it should be dry or sweet, whether oak aging helps" [PécsiBorozó]. This internal fragmentation, combined with a 40% area loss over 5 years [BorEsPiac-2025], represents a genuine **threat to the variety's survival as the district's identity carrier** — exactly the kind of climate–culture coupling flagged by the EU viticulture literature.
- Tasting results at PTE 2025 show a **barrel-aged oxidative risk** (oak tannins not integrating), with **glass/inert aging and amphora** preserving the fruit-forward character best [BorEsPiac-2025].

**Olaszrizling, Tramini, Chardonnay** styles in Pécs are moving to **riper, lower-acid, more tropical profiles** — reducing differentiation from Villány, Szekszárd and Balaton-area peers.

**Reds.** Fuller red styles are the clear stylistic winners in Pécs under warming. Cabernet Franc, Cabernet Sauvignon and Merlot consistently achieve phenolic maturity in sub-Mediterranean Pécs terroir; Kékfrankos shows deeper colour and more consistent tannin ripening than cooler Hungarian peers. Pinot Noir is increasingly marginal: warming pushes Pécs out of its thermal comfort zone.

## 8. Biotic Stressors [OBSERVED]

1. **Flavescence dorée (FD) phytoplasma and its vector *Scaphoideus titanus*.** FD was first detected in Hungary in **2013** and is now present in **21 of 22 wine regions** [BBJ-Hungarian-Wine]. *S. titanus* has spread **from the southern border of Hungary northward and eastward** — meaning southern Transdanubian districts (Pécs, Villány, Szekszárd) have been in the leading edge of the invasion since day one [RG-FD-Hungary]. Experts attribute the proliferation to **warmer winters (climate change), neglected vines, and the EU phase-out of some hazardous pesticides** [Hungary-Winemakers-FD]. Producers describe FD as potentially capable of "wiping out" the industry [Hazard-Herald-FD].
2. **Downy and powdery mildew dynamics.** Wetter, extreme rainfall bursts (see §4) raise infection pressure, while hotter, drier heat spells open windows for powdery mildew. Net effect in Pécs: more unstable, harder to forecast spray schedules.
3. **Grapevine trunk diseases (GTDs: Esca, Botryosphaeria, Eutypa).** Heat- and drought-stressed vines are more susceptible; [GAP] no Pécs-specific incidence data located.
4. **Botrytis cinerea** — historically *beneficial* (noble rot for Cirfandli late-harvest) but with erratic modern autumn conditions it is turning from an asset to a vintage-risk factor.
5. **Wildlife pressure.** Warmer winters and altered phenology increase wild boar, deer and bird damage to vineyards along the Mecsek forest edge — a well-known issue for the Pécs district given its direct interface with the extensive Mecsek forest.

## 9. Abiotic Extremes [OBSERVED]

- **Heatwaves.** 2022 and 2024 summers were the most severe on Hungarian record, with 2022 the "hottest summer since 1901" [Pinke et al. 2024; Euronews 2024]. Pécs/Mecsek, central Danube and the eastern plain have the fastest warming rates [WB-CCKP].
- **Drought.** Compound 2022 drought-heatwave hit after a 2018–2021 dry period [MDPI-Forests-2022]. EU-wide crop losses from the 2022 event were estimated at €13 billion (EU-27) [Pinke et al. 2024]. Pécs' loess soils buffer some drought but iron-rich brown forest soils on slopes are shallow and water-limited.
- **Extreme precipitation.** Increasing trend in daily totals ≥20, ≥30 and ≥40 mm at Pécs [HunGeoBull-EPE]. On the steep Mecsek south-slope topography, this translates to **elevated flash-flood, gully-erosion and vineyard-soil-loss risk**. An orographic signal is detectable in the dense network data.
- **Spring frost.** Mecsek foehn-style downslope winds traditionally buffer frost [bor.hu-HU]. With earlier budburst under warming, however, frost-risk windows shift: the absolute frost date moves later only slowly while budburst advances faster, so net frost exposure risk may rise even as frost frequency declines. [GAP] No quantified frost-risk time series for Pécs located.
- **Hail.** Generally rising concern for southern Hungary; [GAP] no Pécs-specific hail climatology located.

## 10. Indirect Effects

- **Water balance / irrigation.** Pécs viticulture is traditionally **rain-fed**. Increasing evaporative demand + intensified dry spells push toward deficit irrigation, but water licensing, slope hydrology and investment cost are barriers.
- **Soil erosion.** Extreme precipitation bursts on steep south slopes accelerate topsoil loss — a direct threat to the thin rendzinas on Mecsek limestone and the iron-rich browns on Permian sandstone.
- **Phenology–biodiversity coupling.** Forest-edge vineyards on the Mecsek are within a Natura 2000 landscape — management responses (spraying, netting) are constrained.
- **Labour / harvest logistics.** Compressed harvest windows and heatwaves put pressure on the small, family-scale labour pool typical of Pécs.
- **Consumer shift.** Warming favours fuller, riper styles at a moment when European consumers are shifting to lower-alcohol, fresher whites — a direct market mismatch for Pécsi Cirfandli's traditional positioning.

## 11. Future Projections [PROJECTED]

### 11.1 Lakatos & Nagy (2025) — the key reference

Lakatos, L. & Nagy, R. (2025). *"Assessment of historical and future changes in temperature indices for winegrape suitability in Hungarian wine regions (1971–2100)."* **Frontiers in Plant Science** 16: 1481431. DOI: 10.3389/fpls.2025.1481431. (Eszterházy Károly Catholic University, Eger.)

Design: 22 Hungarian wine regions (Pécs included), 14 climate models from the FORESEE bias-corrected ensemble, RCP4.5 and RCP8.5, 1971–2100, four indices — **AGST** (Average Growing Season Temperature, Apr–Oct), **GDD-Winkler** (10 °C base), **Huglin Index** (HI), and **BEDD** (Biologically Effective Degree Days, with 19 °C cap).

Headline aggregate findings:
- **RCP4.5**: mean AGST across Hungarian wine regions rises by approximately **0.6–0.7 °C** over the seven decades to 2100.
- **RCP8.5**: mean AGST rise **exceeds 2.0 °C** across all 22 regions by 2100, with the **smallest change (2.3 °C) in Pannonhalma** (Upper Pannon).
- The most significant AGST rise is expected in Csongrád (Great Plain); the smallest under RCP4.5 in Sopron.
- All Hungarian regions are projected to move **up at least one Huglin climate class** (i.e., Temperate → Warm, or Warm → Warm-temperate to Hot).

**[GAP] Pécs-specific published values.** The PDF text examined does not break out numerical projections for Pécs individually — values are embedded in supplementary tables/figures. For Pécs interpolation, the most defensible inference is:
- Pécs sits in the **southern Transdanubian warming hotspot** flagged by WB-CCKP for the Mecsek, so its warming is *likely above the national average* (i.e., **≥0.7 °C under RCP4.5, ≥2.3 °C and plausibly 2.5–3.0 °C under RCP8.5 by 2100**).
- Under RCP8.5 end-of-century, Pécs Huglin Index is projected to cross **HI > 3000 ("Hot", HI+3)**, entering the class presently occupied by Rhône Valley, northern Spain and central Italy.

### 11.2 Other projections

- **Gaál et al. (Random Forest, Applied Ecology and Environmental Research)** projected that "in the near future (2021–2050) the grapevine regions typical of the southern may expand in greater part of the country, while at the end of the century (2071–2100) only the northern part of the country shows some similarities with the present climate." Hungary remains climatically suitable for quality viticulture, **but varietal structure must change** [Gaál et al.; RG-RandomForest].
- **Morales-Castilla et al. (Climatic Change 2013/2020)**: a 1 °C warming shifts suitability borders ~180 km north — translated to Pécs, a 2 °C warming means today's Pécs climate will be analog to **southern Croatia / coastal Dalmatia** by mid-to-late century.
- **Nature Communications — Climate resilience of European wine regions (2024)** places Hungary among the top-5 highest-exposure EU-27 wine countries, with mean exposure >0.7 [Nat Commun 2024].
- **Drought frequency** over the Great Hungarian Plain will increase under both RCP4.5 and RCP8.5 through 2100; southern Transdanubia including Pécs inherits a weaker but real drying signal [Theor Appl Climatol 2025].
- **Precipitation** projections (EURO-CORDEX bias-corrected ensemble, Hungary): modest change in annual totals but **strong seasonal redistribution** — wetter winters, drier summers, more intense convective events [Sci Total Env 2025].

## 12. Adaptation

**Viticultural (field-level).**
- **Canopy management**: higher, denser canopies to shade grape zones, delayed summer trimming, leaf removal shifted from south to north side of the row, reduced basal leaf removal to avoid sunburn. Discontinuous canopies reduce perspiration while preserving shade [VINEAS].
- **Training system changes**: trend toward higher trellises, cordon conversion, abandoning classical low single-Guyot in the hottest south-facing plots.
- **Rootstock choice**: drought-tolerant rootstocks (110 Richter, 140 Ruggeri, 1103 Paulsen, 41B) replacing vigorous / drought-sensitive ones (SO4, 101-14 Mgt, Schwarzmann) [Chen et al. 2024; Vineyard Magazine].
- **Row orientation**: new plantings increasingly N–S to balance east/west sun exposure and reduce afternoon heat load.
- **Cover crops and mulch**: retaining winter rainfall and reducing soil surface temperatures; particularly important on Pécs' erosion-prone slopes.
- **Deficit irrigation**: introduction in marginal plots where water rights permit.
- **Plot selection**: shift to **higher-elevation Mecsek footslope sites (300–440 m)** — Hosszúhetény, Pécsvárad area — as a cooler refuge.

**Varietal adaptation.**
- **Conserve Cirfandli** via the PTE Research Institute gene bank (~1,500 accessions, world's 6th largest) — this is a globally important asset for the Pannon region [TasteHungary-Pécs].
- Clonal selection of heat- and drought-tolerant Cirfandli clones; adjust picking strategy to **blended picking-date harvests** to balance freshness and spice complexity (recommended by PTE tasting series 2025) [BorEsPiac-2025].
- **Stylistic pivot**: accept 14%+ Cirfandli, emphasize **late-harvest and sweet wine styles** (which retain acid balance better) as signature expressions; protect dry style through amphora and glass aging rather than oak [BorEsPiac-2025; PécsiBorozó].
- **Heritage reds**: re-emphasize **Kadarka** (historical variety) as a heat-tolerant, lighter-styled red that fits both climate and modern "fresher reds" demand.
- Introduction of **Mediterranean varieties** trialled at PTE Research Estate.

**Winery-level.**
- Earlier harvest decisions; night harvest; rapid cooling at reception.
- Acidification (where permitted), co-fermentation with high-acid varieties.
- Inert aging (stainless, glass, amphora) favoured over new oak for Cirfandli [BorEsPiac-2025].
- **Cork and closure quality** — PécsiBorozó specifically highlights premature oxidation as a Cirfandli-specific risk, recommending premium cork or screw cap.

**Disease management.** Rigorous *S. titanus* control (mandatory insecticide treatments under Hungarian plant protection law), vineyard hygiene, removal of abandoned plots, and forecasting network uptake.

**Landscape-level.** Erosion control (terracing, grass strips, hillside drainage) on the steeper Mecsek slopes to cope with intensifying EPEs.

## 13. Economic & Market Dimension

- **Small district, large brand equity.** At ~600–700 ha, Pécs is economically small in Hungarian terms but punches well above its weight in **wine tourism**, anchored by the UNESCO-listed city of Pécs, the **Pécs-Mecseki Borút** wine route, and the PTE research estate [VisitHungary-Pécs; bor.hu-EN]. Some English-language sources describe Pécs as "the most fashionable wine-making region of Hungary thanks to its marketing activities" [WinesOfHungary-Pécs].
- **PDO protection** is secured since the 2012 product specification (2016 amendment) [bor.hu-EN; kormany.hu-Pécs-OEM].
- **Producer structure**: predominantly smallholders with a few cooperative and estate operations; low consolidation means climate-adaptation investments (irrigation, trellis conversion, rootstock replanting) are harder to finance.
- **Tourism as a climate cushion.** Enotourism partly decouples district revenue from yield volatility, but extreme-heat summers reduce visitor comfort and shoulder seasons become the growth windows.
- **Cirfandli as a market differentiator.** The variety's global rarity is the district's strongest unique-selling proposition — and the one most directly threatened by climate-driven stylistic drift and the 40% / 5-year area decline [BorEsPiac-2025]. Loss of Cirfandli would be economically less significant than symbolically catastrophic for Pécs' wine identity.
- **Littke**, Hungary's **first continuously operating sparkling wine (pezsgő) producer**, founded 1859, gives Pécs a secondary identity claim in sparkling wine — ironically positioned to gain from earlier-harvest, higher-acid base-wine potential if harvests shift [TasteHungary-Pécs].

## 14. Knowledge Gaps

1. **Published Pécs-specific projections from Lakatos & Nagy 2025.** The paper includes Pécs in its 22-region analysis but the open-access text does not tabulate Pécs values separately. [HIGH PRIORITY] — request supplementary data from the authors or FORESEE database.
2. **Cirfandli physiology research.** Cirfandli is **strikingly under-researched** relative to its importance to Pécs identity. No peer-reviewed studies located on:
   - Heat-stress thresholds for Cirfandli berry structure (the Ipacs Szabó observation that "extreme heat damages fruit structure more severely than in international varieties" is unquantified).
   - Cirfandli-specific budburst/bloom/véraison phenology models.
   - Berry sugar/acid/pH accumulation curves under Pécs-specific micro-climate.
   - Clonal variation in Cirfandli heat/drought tolerance (despite PTE gene bank holdings).
3. **District-level phenological time series.** No long-term published Pécs phenology dataset for any variety.
4. **District-level yield & harvest-date time series.** Not found in the open literature.
5. **Frost risk trend quantification.** Budburst advance versus last-frost-date advance — unquantified for Pécs.
6. **Hail climatology trends** specific to Mecsek southern footslopes.
7. **Soil erosion rates** under intensifying EPE regime on Pécs' steep slopes — intersected with PDO vineyard boundaries.
8. **Economic impact quantification** (harvest variability → revenue variability) for the ~600 ha district.
9. **Flavescence dorée incidence & economic impact** at Pécs district level.
10. **Consumer reception** of the evolving "riper, higher-alcohol" Cirfandli style — is the traditional off-dry / late-harvest expression still the commercial optimum, or is a dry, barrel-aged modern style commercially viable?
11. **Thin sections of this report (due to sparse source material):** §5 Phenology (quantitative), §8 Biotic Stressors (Pécs-specific), §9 Frost/Hail (Pécs-specific), §11 Pécs-explicit projections.
