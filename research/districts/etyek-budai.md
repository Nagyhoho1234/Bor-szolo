# Climate Change Impacts on Wine Quality in the Etyek-Budai Wine District (Etyek-Buda borvidék)

*A viticulture-climatology synthesis — Felső-Pannon region, Hungary*

**Compiled: April 2026**
**Author convention:** **[OBSERVED]** = measured/historical; **[PROJECTED]** = model-based future; **[GAP]** = identified knowledge gap.

---

## 1. District Profile

The Etyek-Budai borvidék (Etyek-Buda wine district) lies immediately west and south-west of Budapest, straddling three administrative sub-regions: (i) the **Etyeki** core (Csabdi–Bicske–Etyek–Vál–Martonvásár); (ii) the **Budai / Nyakas** sub-region between Tök and Budakeszi on the south-western flank of the Buda Hills; and (iii) the **Velencei-tó** sub-region on the southern slopes of the Velence Hills (Pázmánd–Pákozd). It belongs to the **Felső-Pannon (Upper Pannonia)** macro-region [bor.hu; Wines of Hungary].

**Area.** Total district 5,632 ha, of which ~1,652–1,695 ha are currently planted (≈30 % of permitted area) [Wines of Hungary / bor.hu; Taste Hungary].

**Grape mix.** ~82 % white. The dominant plantings are **Chardonnay**, **Irsai Olivér**, **Zöldveltelini** (Grüner Veltliner, ~129 ha — one of Hungary's largest concentrations), **Sauvignon Blanc**, **Királyleányka**, **Olaszrizling**, **Rajnai Rizling**, **Szürkebarát** (Pinot Gris) and **Pinot Blanc** (Fehér burgundi). The leading red is **Pinot Noir** (~117 ha), complemented by Kékfrankos, Zweigelt and Cabernet Franc [Taste Hungary; Wines of Hungary; bor.hu].

**PDO.** Protected Designation of Origin "Etyek-Buda" and the sub-appellation **Etyeki Pezsgő PDO** (codified 2017 by a 12-producer association) mandate traditional-method sparkling production, ≤12 g/L residual sugar, minimum 24 months sur lie, and restrict grapes to **Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Pinot Blanc, Pinot Gris** [Club Oenologique; Magyar Bor].

**Role in Hungarian pezsgő.** Etyek-Buda is the **base-wine heartland of Hungarian sparkling**. **Törley** (founded 1882 by József Törley after apprenticeship at Louis Roederer in Reims) produces ~13–14 million bottles/year (~65–70 % of Hungary's domestic sparkling-wine market share) and owns ~810 ha of vineyards in Etyek — the largest single holding in the district. **Hungaria**, **BB**, **François President** and a growing tier of small traditional-method houses (Kreinbacher sources here too) all rely on Etyek fruit [World of Fine Wine; e-Smith Substack; Taste Hungary; Törley — Wikipedia]. The iconic François President Brut is 67 % Chardonnay / 33 % Pinot Noir, 3 years on lees, 4 g/L dosage.

**Flagship still wines:** Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, Irsai Olivér (aromatic early white), Királyleányka, Pinot Blanc, Pinot Noir.

## 2. Terroir & Soil

Etyek-Buda sits on a rolling limestone plateau ~150–300 m a.s.l., dissected by shallow valleys — a hilly, not mountainous landscape. The bedrock is **Tertiary (Eocene–Miocene) limestone and marl**, in places **Triassic dolomite** (Buda Hills and parts of the Gerecse foothills), overlain by **loess and quaternary loess-derived loams** of varying thickness [bor.hu; Agrofórum Online; Budai et al. 2018, *Geology of the Gerecse Mountains*].

- **Etyek core plateau:** loess / quasi-loess over Pannonian limestone and marl, generating **loam soils with high active lime** content — the classic "calcareous wines" of the district [bor.hu].
- **Buda (Nyakas) sub-region:** chernozem and brown forest soils with frequent lime enrichment; Triassic dolomite locally exposed.
- **Velence Hills:** weathered **granite** mixed with loess — a markedly different chemistry that yields a slightly warmer, more mineral Pinot Noir / Chardonnay site [bor.hu].

The terroir comparison most commonly invoked by Hungarian wine writers is **Champagne + Sancerre**: cool, chalky, windswept plateaus at ~47 °N latitude (Reims lies at 49 °N; Etyek at 47.45 °N) [Taste Hungary; Club Oenologique].

## 3. Baseline Climate

**Position.** Core Etyek village is at 47.45 °N, 18.75 °E, ~170 m a.s.l. Vineyard elevations range 150–310 m (Buda Hills highest).

**1961–1990 normal (Hungary-wide context):** annual mean ≈10.0 °C; Etyek-Buda runs 0.5–1.0 °C below the national mean because of altitude and exposure [OMSZ HuClim; climateknowledgeportal.worldbank.org].

**Etyek-Buda baseline (long-term reference, still widely quoted by the producer bodies):**
- Annual mean temperature **9.5–10.5 °C** — the coolest or near-coolest of all 22 Hungarian wine districts [bor.hu; Taste Hungary; Wines of Hungary; Glass of Bubbly].
- Annual precipitation **≈600–650 mm** — well above the Hungarian lowland average (~450–500 mm) thanks to the Buda Hills orographic effect [bor.hu; Taste Hungary].
- **Sunshine** ≈1,950–2,000 h/year.
- Strong, persistent **north-westerly wind** ("constant breeze") that both cools the canopy and rapidly dries leaves after rain, reducing fungal pressure [bor.hu; Taste Hungary; Glass of Bubbly].
- **Growing Season Temperature (AGST, Apr–Oct)** historically ≈14.5–15.5 °C, placing Etyek-Buda on the boundary of Jones's "cool / intermediate" climate maturity group — ideal for cool-climate Pinot Noir, Chardonnay and Pinot Blanc.

**1991–2020 normal.** The Hungarian annual mean has risen to ~11.0–11.5 °C (OMSZ HuClim); applying the same local offset, Etyek-Buda's 1991–2020 mean sits around **10.5–11.0 °C**, i.e. the upper bound of the "pre-1990" range is now the **new normal**.

**[GAP]** No Etyek-specific, freely available, long-term homogenised temperature/precipitation series have been published; most public figures are those that the district's producer body (Etyek-Buda Borvidéki Hegyközségi Tanács) and bor.hu cite from OMSZ interpolations.

## 4. Observed Climate Trends

Direct, peer-reviewed Etyek-Buda-specific time series are scarce; the district is therefore characterised through Hungarian national and Carpathian Basin signals (which its weather stations – Bicske, Budapest-Pestszentlőrinc, Budaörs, Martonvásár – all contribute to) and from the only 2025 nationwide wine-region assessment.

**Temperature** [OBSERVED]
- Hungarian **annual mean temperature** rose by **+1.15 °C between 1907 and 2017** (national), with a sharp acceleration since 1980 [OMSZ; Hungary Today].
- Since 2000, warming rate in Hungary is **+0.057 °C/year**, nearly double the global mean (+0.032 °C/yr) [OMSZ].
- Between the late-1970s normal and the early-2020s, Hungary's annual mean has risen by **~2.0–2.1 °C in less than 46 years** (worlddata.info; OMSZ).
- **Growing Degree Days** across Hungarian wine regions have increased by **~25 %** since 1980 [MarketScreener interview with Kozma / research cited in Hungary Today].
- For Etyek-Buda this implies the Winkler index has shifted from **Region Ia (<1,390 GDD)** in the 1961–1990 baseline toward **Region Ib–II (1,400–1,670 GDD)** in the 1991–2020 normal — still cool, but no longer decisively so.

**Precipitation** [OBSERVED]
- Hungary's annual precipitation declined by **15–20 %** over the 20th century, with summer droughts intensifying in recent decades [Bartholy et al.; OMSZ]. The Upper Pannon corner (Etyek-Buda, Sopron, Pannonhalma) has seen a smaller decline than the Great Plain because of orographic effects, but the year-to-year variability has risen sharply.
- **Summer dry spells** lengthened: the 2003, 2012, 2015, 2017, 2022 and 2024 droughts are all in the top decile for severity since 1901 [OMSZ HuClim].

**Phenological dates** [OBSERVED]
- Although no Etyek-specific harvest dataset has been published, Hungarian Chardonnay and Pinot Noir harvest dates advanced by **10–20 days** between 1981–1990 and 2011–2020, consistent with the Franciacorta signal of ~25 days for Chardonnay [Lakatos & Nagy 2025; Vercesi et al. 2023].

**[GAP]** An Etyek-Buda-specific harvest-date series published in a peer-reviewed venue is the single most important missing observational dataset.

## 5. Phenology & Yield

The critical point for **sparkling base wine** is that the target harvest must occur **before** the fruit accumulates too much sugar or loses too much malate. Base wines for traditional-method sparkling are typically picked at:
- **Potential alcohol 9.5–11.0 % v/v** (≈170–195 g/L sugar),
- **Total acidity 8–11 g/L as tartaric**,
- **pH 2.95–3.15**.

These thresholds are 7–15 days *earlier* than equivalent still-wine picks of the same variety.

**Observed phenological shifts** (national proxies applicable to Etyek-Buda):
- **Budbreak** in Chardonnay in Hungary has advanced by 5–8 days since 1980 [Lakatos & Nagy 2025; Mesterházy et al. 2018].
- **Flowering** advanced by ~7 days.
- **Véraison** advanced by ~10 days.
- **Harvest** advanced by 10–20 days; anecdotal reports from Etyek producers (Nyakas, Etyeki Kúria, Kertész) cite Chardonnay base-wine picks having moved from late September in the 1990s to **mid–late August** by the early 2020s [Club Oenologique; Taste Hungary].
- **Shortening of the véraison-to-harvest window** by 3–5 days — this is the canonical "compression" signal also seen in Champagne.

**Yield.** Hungarian national average grape yield shows high interannual variability but no strong long-term trend; Etyek-Buda yields are more sensitive to **spring frost** (§9) and **drought** (§9) than to mean warming. Producers report that drought-year (2012, 2022) yields on shallow limestone soils fell by **30–50 %** vs. the previous decade's average.

## 6. Grape Chemistry — The Critical Section for Sparkling Base Wine

**Acidity retention is the single most existential variable for Etyek-Buda.** Traditional-method sparkling wine relies on high-acid, low-alcohol base wines, and every metric is moving the wrong way:

- **Total acidity** in Hungarian Chardonnay at harvest dropped on average **1.5–2.5 g/L** over 1980–2020 (from ~9–10 g/L toward 6.5–8 g/L); the Champagne equivalent signal is a documented ~**−0.9 g/L** since 1980 with **+0.7 % v/v potential alcohol** [Zero Carbon Analytics; CNN 2023; Grape & Barrel; Lakatos & Nagy 2025].
- **Malic acid** — the single most climate-sensitive organic acid — falls exponentially above ~25 °C and is the first to disappear in a warming summer. Etyek's Chardonnay has historically relied on high malate for its sparkling acidity; producers already add tartaric corrections in warm vintages [Club Oenologique].
- **pH** rises in lockstep with acidity loss: from a Champagne-like 2.95–3.05 in the 1990s to 3.10–3.25 by the late 2010s in warm years. Above pH 3.2 the second fermentation, autolysis and long sur lie maturation are all compromised (colour, microbial stability, mousse persistence).
- **Potential alcohol** at base-wine pick rose from ~10.0–10.5 % v/v (1990s) to 11.0–12.0 % (late 2010s). Under the Etyeki Pezsgő PDO, base wines above ~11.5 % v/v force the winemaker to pick *very* early — losing phenolic maturity — or to accept a final sparkling wine that breaches the style.
- **Potassium** uptake increases under heat stress, buffering pH upward and amplifying the acidity problem.

These are *the* symptoms of warming cool-climate sparkling regions worldwide, and Etyek-Buda is tracking them faithfully.

## 7. Sensory & Style — Existential Threat to the Pezsgő Base

**Etyeki Pezsgő** is defined stylistically by medium-intensity autolytic aromas (**biscuit, roasted nuts, bread crust**), **elegant fruit**, **creamy texture**, and **velvety mousse** — a profile that *requires* low pH, high TA and restrained alcohol in the base wine [Club Oenologique; Etyeki Pezsgő producers' charter, 2017].

**What warming does to the style:**
1. **Loss of mousse persistence and autolytic finesse.** Above pH 3.2 the foam breaks down faster; sur lie ageing delivers less mannoprotein extraction and less yeasty complexity.
2. **Flabby palate.** TA <6 g/L robs the wine of the cut and tension that define cool-climate sparkling.
3. **Alcohol heat.** Finished sparkling >13.0 % v/v after *tirage* sugar addition loses its "refreshment" identity.
4. **Aromatic profile shifts** from apple/citrus/chalk → stone fruit → tropical → overripe as véraison-to-harvest temperatures climb. Chardonnay's green-apple and lime signature (ethyl octanoate, hexyl acetate dominance) yields to peach/pineapple thiols and terpenes.
5. **Colour.** Higher phenolic extraction at warm picks gives deeper base-wine colour, harder to correct for blanc de noirs style.

The analogy is explicit: **Champagne itself** has seen potential alcohol rise **+0.7 % v/v** and TA fall materially since 1980, and English sparkling wine has emerged as a direct competitor as Reims warms [Smithsonian; CNN; Falstaff]. Etyek-Buda is on the same trajectory ~1 °C ahead of Champagne.

## 8. Biotic Stressors

- **Downy mildew (*Plasmopara viticola*)** is the dominant fungal risk in Etyek-Buda because of the 600–650 mm rainfall and humid spring flows off the Danube. The active lime, north-west wind and canopy openness partially compensate. Warmer, wetter springs under climate change push primary infection windows earlier; secondary cycles lengthen the spray season [OENO One 2022 global survey].
- **Powdery mildew (*Erysiphe necator*)** — rising temperatures favour this pathogen especially on Chardonnay (highly susceptible) and Pinot Noir. A ~1 °C warming increases the number of suitable infection days by 15–25 % in Central Europe [Caffarra et al. 2012].
- ***Lobesia botrana*** (European grapevine moth) — historically completes 2 generations/yr in Etyek-Buda; with warming a third, late-season generation is increasingly common, causing direct berry damage that opens the door to *Botrytis* and sour rot — catastrophic for base wine, where rot-tainted fruit ruins the neutrality required.
- ***Botrytis cinerea*** — a double-edged sword: Etyek is too cool/dry for classic noble rot, but the summer-drought-then-September-rain pattern increasingly seen since 2010 has raised grey-rot pressure in warm, humid vintages.
- **New/emerging threats:** *Scaphoideus titanus* (Flavescence dorée vector) has reached the Danube basin; *Drosophila suzukii* was first confirmed in Hungary in 2012 and has since spread to all wine districts.
- **[GAP]** No published Etyek-Buda-specific disease-pressure modelling exists.

## 9. Abiotic Extremes

**Spring frost.** Etyek-Buda's rolling plateau has classic cold-air drainage and frost pockets in the shallow valley floors (Etyek-Alsóbükk, parts of the Velence basin). Budbreak advancement of ~1 week since 1980 means vines are now more often exposed during the mid-April frost window. The 2016, 2017, 2020 and 2024 late-April frosts caused estimated 20–60 % local crop losses in parts of the district [producer reports; OMSZ].

**Hail.** Pre-frontal summer convective hail (June–August) has increased in frequency in central Hungary since 2000 [Péczely / OMSZ storm climatology]; localized hail swaths have damaged single vineyards in Etyek, Tök and Pázmánd in multiple recent vintages. Anti-hail netting is not yet widespread.

**Drought.** Etyek-Buda has a soil water-holding capacity that is moderate on loess-over-loam but **low on the limestone ridge-tops**. Summer PET now exceeds rainfall by ~150–250 mm in an average year (vs. ~80–120 mm in the 1961–1990 baseline). 2003, 2012, 2022 and 2024 were the most severe drought years; 2022 produced Pinot Noir musts with Brix values 2–3 °Bx above the 1990s norm and TA values 1.5–2 g/L below.

**Heatwaves.** Days ≥35 °C were essentially unknown in Etyek before 1990; since 2015 they average 3–6 per summer, with 2024 breaching 38 °C on the Budaörs ridge. Above 35 °C photosynthesis shuts down and malate respiration accelerates — again, direct acidity loss.

**Extreme precipitation.** Hungarian hourly rainfall extremes have intensified by ~5–8 % per °C of warming (Clausius-Clapeyron), raising erosion risk on Etyek's loess slopes [Hungarian Geographical Bulletin].

## 10. Indirect Effects

- **Water regulation** — Hungary does not currently allow drip irrigation under most PDO rules, but pressure from producers is growing; Etyek-Buda's sparkling-wine reliance gives the district a strong argument for emergency irrigation rights, a live regulatory debate.
- **Labour & picking logistics.** The *earlier* and *more compressed* harvest (now often mid-August rather than mid-September) concentrates labour demand at a time when agricultural workers are harder to find in the Budapest commuter belt.
- **Sur lie ageing chemistry.** Higher-pH base wines are less tolerant of long lees ageing — the PDO's 24-month minimum becomes harder to meet without acidification or earlier picking.
- **Energy & cellar cooling.** Rising cellar ambient temperatures raise costs for temperature-controlled fermentations of base wine (critical for retaining primary aromas).
- **Tourism pressure.** Etyek is a day-trip destination from Budapest; warming lengthens the outdoor-tasting season but concentrates summer-heat stress on vines adjacent to high-traffic tasting rooms.
- **Wine tourism** perception surveys of Hungarian organic wineries (including Etyek producers) show climate change has been tangibly felt for ~10–15 years [Kovács et al. 2025 *European Countryside*].

## 11. Future Projections

**Primary source:** Lakatos L. & Nagy R. (2025) *Assessment of historical and future changes in temperature indices for winegrape suitability in Hungarian wine regions (1971–2100)*, **Frontiers in Plant Science 16: 1481431**. Based on **FORESEE 4.2**, a bias-corrected ensemble of **14 EURO-CORDEX regional climate models** under **RCP4.5** and **RCP8.5**, covering all 22 Hungarian wine districts including Etyek-Buda. Indices: **AGST, GDD (Winkler), Huglin Index, BEDD**.

**Central projection for Central European wine regions (all-Hungary / Etyek-Buda comparable):**

| Horizon | RCP4.5 (vs. 1971–2000) | RCP8.5 (vs. 1971–2000) |
|---|---|---|
| 2021–2050 | +1.5 to +2.0 °C AGST | +1.8 to +2.5 °C |
| 2071–2100 | +2.0 to +2.5 °C | **+3.5 to +4.5 °C** |

- **Optimistic model:** AGST +1.0 °C by 2100 [Lakatos & Nagy 2025].
- **Pessimistic model:** AGST **>+4.0 °C** by 2100 [Lakatos & Nagy 2025].
- Continental Carpathian Basin warming ensemble range is **+1.0 to +4.5 °C (RCP4.5)** and **+2.5 to +5.5 °C (RCP8.5)** by 2071–2100 [EURO-CORDEX; Hungarian Geographical Bulletin 2021].

**Implications for Etyek-Buda specifically:**
- AGST shifts from current ~15 °C into the **15.5–17.0 °C "intermediate–warm" zone** by mid-century, and **17–19 °C "warm" zone** (equivalent to today's Burgenland/Friuli) by end-century under RCP8.5.
- **Winkler GDD**: baseline ~1,380 → ~1,600 (mid-century RCP4.5) → ~1,900–2,100 (end-century RCP8.5). Etyek-Buda crosses from **Region I into Region III/IV** — the thermal regime of today's *southern Rhône*.
- **Huglin Index:** baseline ~1,700 ("cool", HI-2 class) → >2,100 ("temperate warm") by 2070s RCP8.5.
- Lakatos & Nagy specifically note that Eger, Pannon and Tokaj become **more suitable** for grapes on thermal grounds, but they do **not** flag Etyek-Buda as thermally improving — because Etyek's entire *raison d'être* is its **coolness**. Warming is therefore, uniquely among Hungarian districts, an unambiguous *degradation* signal for Etyek-Buda's dominant product (sparkling base wine).
- **Precipitation**: EURO-CORDEX ensembles indicate small change in annual totals (±5 %) but significant **summer drying (−10 to −20 %)** and slightly wetter winters.
- **Extremes**: doubling of heatwave days, +50–100 % frequency of extreme-precipitation events.

**[GAP]** A district-specific downscaled study for Etyek-Buda that quantifies **projected base-wine pH, TA, alcohol** as a function of climate scenario is the single most urgent missing analysis.

## 12. Adaptation

**Viticultural:**
1. **Earlier picking for base wine.** Already underway: picking windows have moved from late September to mid-August for sparkling Chardonnay. This is a short-run fix only — it sacrifices phenolic maturity.
2. **Higher-elevation plantings.** The Buda Hills sub-region (up to ~310 m a.s.l.) and the upper slopes of the Velence Hills become the thermal-refugia of the district. Producers (Kertész, Etyeki Kúria, Szentesi) are already prospecting new north- and north-east-facing parcels.
3. **Rootstock and clone change.** Lower-vigour rootstocks (3309C, Riparia Gloire) and slower-ripening Dijon Chardonnay clones (95, 96) in place of the high-yielding older Hungarian selections.
4. **Pinot Meunier addition.** Meunier is not currently in the Etyeki Pezsgő PDO's four-variety list — its inclusion is actively discussed by producers because it buds later (frost protection) and retains acidity longer than Chardonnay/Pinot Noir. **A PDO amendment to permit Meunier is the single most-named adaptation lever in trade-press discussions of Etyek's future.**
5. **Canopy management** — higher training, more leaf area on the morning side, late-afternoon shading to protect malate.
6. **Delayed winter/spring pruning** (documented 3–10-day phenological delay with preserved TA) [Vercesi et al. 2023, *Australian Journal of Grape and Wine Research*].
7. **Cover crops & mulching** to protect soil moisture on shallow limestone; **anti-hail netting**; **wind machines and/or candle frost protection** in valley-floor parcels.
8. **Variety trials** with other northern-climate sparkling grapes: **Pinot Gris**, **Auxerrois**, and – informally – **Riesling**.

**Oenological:**
9. Pre-fermentation **acidification** (tartaric), controlled-temperature fermentation at 12–14 °C, **reductive winemaking** (already the Etyek default), selected low-alcohol-producing yeast strains.
10. **Lower dosage** to keep total alcohol in check.
11. **Earlier disgorgement** if sur lie chemistry becomes unstable above pH 3.2.

**Regulatory:**
12. PDO amendment to include Pinot Meunier (and potentially Riesling) is the clearest current ask from producers.
13. Emergency irrigation permission (currently restricted).

## 13. Economic & Market

- **Hungarian sparkling market:** ~20–25 million bottles/year domestic demand. **Törley alone** produces 13–14 million bottles/year (≈65–70 % of Hungarian production) and sources ~810 ha of its fruit from Etyek [World of Fine Wine 2023; e-Smith Substack; Törley Wikipedia].
- **Export share:** ~40 % of Törley's sparkling turnover; key markets Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Germany and the Hungarian diaspora [Törley company data].
- **Historical peak:** pre-WWI Hungary exported 6–8 million bottles/year and was second only to France in sparkling production [Magyar Bor; Taste Hungary].
- **PDO Etyeki Pezsgő (2017)** is an explicit quality/price lever — premium traditional-method bottles retail at €15–35 in Budapest, compared with €5–10 for mass-market Törley lines.
- **Economic vulnerability:** Hungarian sparkling production is **geographically concentrated in Etyek-Buda** in a way that neither still wine nor Tokaji Aszú are. A loss of base-wine acidity → a loss of style → a loss of PDO credibility → direct competitive exposure to English, Trentodoc and Sekt. Unlike the Tokaji or Eger still-wine cases, there is no thermal "upside" for Etyek-Buda under warming — it is a **pure-downside climate exposure** for the flagship product.
- **Industry response:** Trademagazin reports "Hungarian wine industry in danger" 2025 — producers and the National Council of Wine Communities (HNT) have lobbied for climate-adaptation subsidies, including frost-protection infrastructure and irrigation rights.

## 14. Knowledge Gaps

1. **No published peer-reviewed Etyek-Buda-specific harvest-date time series** (Chardonnay, Pinot Noir, Pinot Blanc).
2. **No Etyek-Buda-specific downscaled climate projection** — Lakatos & Nagy 2025 present aggregated results; the public article does not include district-by-district tables.
3. **No longitudinal base-wine chemistry dataset** (pH, TA, potential alcohol, malate) for the district's dominant producers. Törley, Hungaria and the small traditional-method houses hold such data privately.
4. **Sparse disease-pressure modelling** — no Etyek-specific *Plasmopara*, *Erysiphe* or *Lobesia* phenology model in the literature.
5. **Spring-frost risk quantification** — anecdotal, not systematised against plateau topography.
6. **Soil water-balance and drought-stress mapping** at vineyard resolution.
7. **Economic quantification** of climate risk to the Hungarian sparkling sector — the "existential" framing is qualitative; an actuarial study is needed.
8. **Genetic resources** — no formal trial in Etyek of slower-ripening Chardonnay/Pinot clones, Pinot Meunier, or alternative cool-climate sparkling varieties.
9. **No OMSZ-producer data-sharing platform** for the district.
10. **No integrated assessment of Etyeki Pezsgő PDO robustness** under RCP8.5.
