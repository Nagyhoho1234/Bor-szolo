# Crop Evapotranspiration: Guidelines for Computing Crop Water Requirements

- **Authors:** Richard G. Allen, Luis S. Pereira, Dirk Raes, Martin Smith
- **Year:** 1998
- **Journal:** FAO Irrigation and Drainage Paper 56 (FAO, Rome)
- **DOI:** N/A (FAO publication)
- **Access:** Open access at [FAO](https://www.fao.org/4/x0490e/x0490e00.htm); [PDF](http://www.climasouth.eu/sites/default/files/FAO%2056.pdf)
- **Status:** FULL TEXT AVAILABLE (open access)

## Summary

The definitive reference for computing reference evapotranspiration (ET0) using the FAO Penman-Monteith method. This is the sole standard method recommended by FAO for ET0 calculation.

## Key Content

- FAO Penman-Monteith equation requiring: radiation, air temperature, humidity, wind speed
- Hargreaves-Samani simplified method for temperature-only stations
- Crop coefficients (Kc) for various crops including grapevine
- Step-by-step calculation procedures
- Guidelines for missing data situations

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Essential for Direction 5 (vineyard water balance). ET0 can be computed at stations with full data (Penman-Monteith) or temperature-only stations (Hargreaves-Samani). Combined with soil moisture sensor data and soil property maps, this enables the spatially distributed daily water balance model.
