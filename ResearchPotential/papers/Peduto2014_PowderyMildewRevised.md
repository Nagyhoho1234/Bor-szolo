# Effect of High Temperature and Exposure Time on Erysiphe necator Growth and Reproduction: Revisions to the UC Davis Powdery Mildew Risk Index

- **Authors:** Francesca Peduto, Philip Backup, Elizabeth K. Hand, Charles N. Janousek, W. Douglas Gubler
- **Year:** 2013 (often cited as 2014)
- **Journal:** Plant Disease, 97, 1438-1447
- **DOI:** 10.1094/PDIS-01-13-0039-RE
- **Access:** [APS Journals](https://apsjournals.apsnet.org/doi/10.1094/PDIS-01-13-0039-RE) (paywalled); [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/239939354)
- **Status:** PAYWALLED - abstract available

## Abstract

Revised the high-temperature threshold of the Gubler-Thomas powdery mildew risk index. Found that the pathogen (Erysiphe necator) survived, grew, and reproduced at higher temperatures than previously reported. Exposure time was as important as temperature in defining lethal and sublethal effects.

## Key Findings

- E. necator tolerates higher temperatures than originally assumed
- Temperature and exposure duration have multiplicative interactions on all life stages
- Original GT index high-temperature threshold needed upward revision
- Combined controlled environment studies with field trials

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Updated parameters for applying the powdery mildew risk index with the 50-station network data.
