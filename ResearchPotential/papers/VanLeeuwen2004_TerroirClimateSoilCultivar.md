## Abstract

The three main components of terroir -- soil, climate, and cultivar -- were studied simultaneously. Vine development and berry composition of non-irrigated Vitis vinifera cultivars (Merlot, Cabernet franc, Cabernet Sauvignon) were compared on a gravelly soil, a soil with heavy clay subsoil, and a sandy soil with a water table within root reach.

## Key Findings

- Climate, soil, and cultivar all significantly influence vine behavior and wine quality
- Soil water supply is the primary soil-related driver of terroir expression
- Climate (vintage effect) explains much of the inter-annual variation
- Cultivar mediates the expression of environmental factors
- Quantified the relative importance of each terroir component

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Provides the theoretical framework for understanding how climate data from the 50-station network interacts with existing soil maps (Pasztor et al.) and variety-specific responses (Furmint, Harslevelyu) to define Tokaj terroir.
