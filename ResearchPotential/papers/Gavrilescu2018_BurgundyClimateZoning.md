# Climate Zoning of the Burgundy Winegrowing Region

- **Authors:** Catinca Gavrilescu, Yves Richard, Daniel Joly, Benjamin Bois
- **Year:** 2018
- **Journal:** E3S Web of Conferences, 50, 01003
- **DOI:** 10.1051/e3sconf/20185001003
- **Access:** Open access at [E3S Web of Conferences](https://www.e3s-conferences.org/articles/e3sconf/abs/2018/25/e3sconf_terroircongress2018_01003/e3sconf_terroircongress2018_01003.html) and [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/327157240)
- **Status:** FULL TEXT AVAILABLE (open access, CC BY 4.0)

## Abstract

Daily minimum and maximum temperatures from 64 weather stations across Burgundy's 9 wine production subregions were spatially interpolated on a grid with 75 m resolution over a 41,751 ha area. Five agricultural climatic indices were modeled and spatialized: heat stress days (>=35C), frost risk days (<=-1C), mid-flowering, mid-veraison, and theoretical grape maturity dates.

## Key Methodology

- 64 weather stations across 9 subregions
- Regression-kriging at 75 m resolution
- GFV phenological model for Chardonnay and Pinot noir
- Six phenological categories for pixel classification
- Analysis period: 2011-2015

## Key Findings

- Notable differences between northern and southern Burgundy
- Potential variation of 7 to 15 days in phenological and maturity occurrence dates
- Cote de Nuits and Cote de Beaune: early phenological timing
- Chablis and Cote Chalonnaise: elevated frost risks
- Heat stress concentrated in warmest southern subregions

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

This is the closest scale analog to Tokaj. Burgundy: 64 stations, 41,751 ha, 75 m resolution. Tokaj: ~50 stations, ~11,000 ha -- even denser coverage, enabling finer resolution (25-50 m). The regression-kriging + GFV methodology is directly transferable.
