## Summary

This seminal book introduced the concept of Biologically Effective Degree Days (BEDD), which are growing degree days capped at 19C. This cap reflects the biological principle that vine development does not accelerate indefinitely with temperature -- the effectiveness of heat accumulation plateaus above approximately 19C.

## BEDD Definition

BEDD = Sum of: min(Tmean - 10, 9) for days where Tmean > 10C

This differs from standard Winkler GDD which has no upper cap. BEDD better reflects the biological reality that extremely hot days are not proportionally more beneficial for vine development.

## Key Contributions

- Comprehensive analysis of environmental factors affecting grapevine growing and wine quality
- Introduction of BEDD as a refined alternative to Winkler GDD
- Emphasis on nighttime temperatures and diurnal range
- Australian focus but with global applicability
- Became a viticultural classic

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

BEDD is one of the standard indices to compute at all 50 stations alongside HI and Winkler GDD. The cap at 19C is particularly relevant for assessing climate change impacts, as warming beyond this threshold provides diminishing returns for vine development.
