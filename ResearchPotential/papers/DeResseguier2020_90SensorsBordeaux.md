# Temperature Variability at Local Scale in the Bordeaux Area: Relations With Environmental Factors and Impact on Vine Phenology

- **Authors:** Laure De Rességuier, Sylvie Mary, Renan Le Roux, Théo Petitjean, Hervé Quénol, Cornelis van Leeuwen
- **Year:** 2020
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Plant Science, 11, 515
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fpls.2020.00515
- **Access:** Open access at [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/plant-science/articles/10.3389/fpls.2020.00515/full)
- **Status:** FULL TEXT AVAILABLE

## Abstract

This study examined spatial temperature variations across approximately 19,233 hectares in the Saint-Emilion, Pomerol, and satellite appellations of Bordeaux, France, using a network of 90 temperature sensors deployed within grapevine canopies from 2012 to 2018 (seven vintages).

## Key Findings

- **Spatial amplitude of up to 10C** in daily minimum temperatures on a given day
- Winkler index varied by approximately 320 degree-days within single growing seasons
- Elevation, slope, exposure, latitude, and longitude significantly affected temperature distribution
- Anticyclonic weather conditions (clear skies, calm air) produced greater spatial temperature variation than cyclonic conditions
- Temperature variations directly influenced vine development timing (budbreak, flowering, veraison)
- Created phenological maps demonstrating how thermal conditions affected flowering and veraison dates across the region

## Methodology

- 90 iButton temperature sensors deployed inside grapevine canopies
- 60 reference plots planted with Merlot monitored for phenological stages (budbreak, flowering, veraison)
- Seven vintages (2012-2018) analyzed
- Statistical analysis of temperature-environment relationships
- Phenological mapping based on thermal conditions

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

This is the closest methodological template for Tokaj's 50-station network. The study demonstrates that dense sensor networks reveal mesoclimatic variability invisible to gridded products. Tokaj's volcanic terrain may produce similar or greater spatial amplitude in minimum temperatures.
