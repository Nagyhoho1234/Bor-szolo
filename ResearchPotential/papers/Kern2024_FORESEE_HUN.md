## Abstract

The FORESEE database provides open-access climate information for Central Europe covering 1951-2100. It offers daily meteorological variables at 0.1 x 0.1 degree spatial resolution. FORESEE-HUN v1.0 is the Hungary-specific version using HUCLIM gridded data for 1971-2021 and 28 bias- and discontinuity-corrected projections for 2022-2100.

## Key Dataset Characteristics

- **Temporal coverage:** 150 years (1951-2100)
- **Variables:** Maximum/minimum temperature, precipitation, shortwave radiation, daylight vapor pressure deficit
- **Resolution:** Daily, 0.1 x 0.1 degree grid (~11 km)
- **Scenarios:** RCP 4.5 and RCP 8.5, 14 regional climate models from EURO-CORDEX
- **Historical source (HUN):** HUCLIM gridded dataset from Hungarian Meteorological Service
- **Status:** Open access climate service

## Key Findings

- Under RCP4.5: temperature increase 1.5-1.7C by 2071-2100 vs 1991-2020
- Precipitation changes show considerable regional variation (-1.6 to 6.9%)
- Agricultural impact: 15.4% (RCP4.5) and 28.9% (RCP8.5) mean winter wheat yield loss by 2071-2100
- Growing season onset advances 9.1 days (RCP4.5) and 19.8 days (RCP8.5) by 2100
- Selecting representative models can produce misleading results -- ensemble approaches emphasized

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

This is the primary gridded climate dataset for Hungary. The 50-station network provides ground-truth for evaluating how well FORESEE-HUN captures mesoclimatic variability in Tokaj's complex terrain (Manuscript 4). Also provides RCP4.5/8.5 projections for Direction 7 (climate change trend detection). Bias correction using station data is a key methodological contribution.
