# Development of a Grape Powdery Mildew Risk Index

- **Authors:** C.S. Thomas, W.D. Gubler, G. Leavitt
- **Year:** 1994
- **Journal:** Phytopathology (conference proceedings / technical reports)
- **DOI:** N/A
- **Access:** Referenced in UC IPM resources and subsequent publications
- **Status:** HISTORIC REFERENCE - not easily accessible as standalone publication

## Summary

The Gubler-Thomas (GT) risk assessment model is a temperature-based index for grape powdery mildew (Erysiphe necator) risk prediction. It uses temperature thresholds to classify daily risk levels.

## Key Model Description

- Based on the observation that powdery mildew epidemics are driven by moderate temperatures (21-30C)
- Risk categories based on consecutive hours in favorable temperature range
- Originally specified a lethal high-temperature threshold (later revised by Peduto et al. 2014)
- Widely used in California and internationally

## Risk Assessment

- Low risk: few consecutive hours in 21-30C range
- Moderate risk: extended periods in favorable range
- High risk: sustained favorable conditions
- Risk reduction: extended periods above lethal threshold

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

The powdery mildew risk index requires only hourly temperature, making it applicable across all 50 stations. Computing daily risk indices and spatially interpolating them produces vineyard-scale powdery mildew risk maps for Direction 2.
