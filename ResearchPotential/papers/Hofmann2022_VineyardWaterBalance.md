# Downscaling of Climate Change Scenarios for a High-Resolution, Site-Specific Assessment of Drought Stress Risk for Two Viticultural Regions with Heterogeneous Landscapes

- **Authors:** Marco Hofmann, Claudia Volosciuk, Martin Dubrovsky, Douglas Maraun, Hans R. Schultz
- **Year:** 2022
- **Journal:** Earth System Dynamics, 13, 911-934
- **DOI:** 10.5194/esd-13-911-2022
- **Access:** Open access at [Copernicus/ESD](https://esd.copernicus.org/articles/13/911/2022/)
- **Status:** FULL TEXT AVAILABLE

## Abstract

A vineyard water balance model was developed that accounts for heterogeneity in soil water storage capacity, evapotranspiration as a function of slope and aspect, and viticultural management. Fed with soil maps, DEM, and EU vineyard register data, it enabled drought stress risk assessment at individual vineyard plot resolution.

## Key Findings

- **Historical (1959-1988):** ~6% of Rheingau vineyards experienced significant drought stress
- **Recent (2014-2018):** 2018 showed 876 mm reference ET (highest since 1951), affecting ~13% of Rheingau
- **Projected (RCP8.5, 2041-2070):** 10-30% of Rheingau vineyards affected; steep slopes and low water capacity sites at highest risk
- Drought risk varies substantially between vineyard sites
- Winter precipitation increases offset by summer decreases and elevated ET
- Temperature warming of 2.5-5.6C by 2100 under RCP8.5

## Methodology

- Plot-scale water balance model with ~24,858 individual vineyard plots
- Stochastic weather generator for synthetic climate series
- 10 regional climate models for projections
- Drought threshold: soil water content below 15% available water capacity
- DEM for slope/aspect effects on ET

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Best methodological template for Direction 5 (vineyard water balance). Directly transferable to Tokaj using the 50-station network, Hungarian soil maps from Pasztor et al., and FORESEE-HUN projections. The plot-scale resolution approach is ideal for Tokaj's ~11,000 ha vineyard area.
