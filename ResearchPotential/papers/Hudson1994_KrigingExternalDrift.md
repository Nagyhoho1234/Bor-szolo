# Mapping Temperature Using Kriging with External Drift: Theory and an Example from Scotland

- **Authors:** Gilles Hudson, Hans Wackernagel
- **Year:** 1994
- **Journal:** International Journal of Climatology, 14, 77-91
- **DOI:** 10.1002/joc.3370140107
- **Access:** [Wiley](https://rmets.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/joc.3370140107) (paywalled); [PDF at Mines Paris](https://cg.minesparis.psl.eu/bibliotheque/public/HUDSON_Publication_02316.pdf)
- **Status:** PDF AVAILABLE via Mines Paris

## Abstract

Demonstrated the application of kriging with external drift (KED) to mapping January mean temperatures in Scotland, exploiting the correlation with elevation.

## Key Contributions

- KED produces kriged temperature estimates that reproduce correlation with elevation
- Theoretical framework for incorporating auxiliary variables (elevation) into spatial interpolation
- Foundational paper for regression-kriging approaches in climate mapping
- Demonstrated that elevation explains most temperature variability

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Theoretical foundation for the regression-kriging approach used across all spatial interpolation in the research plan.
