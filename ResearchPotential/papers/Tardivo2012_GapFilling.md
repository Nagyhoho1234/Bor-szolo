# A Dynamic Method for Gap Filling in Daily Temperature Datasets

- **Authors:** Gianfranco Tardivo, Antonio Berti
- **Year:** 2012
- **Journal:** Journal of Applied Meteorology and Climatology, 51(6), 1079-1095
- **DOI:** 10.1175/JAMC-D-11-0117.1
- **Access:** [AMS Journals](https://journals.ametsoc.org/view/journals/apme/51/6/jamc-d-11-0117.1.xml); [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/258658976)
- **Status:** ACCESSIBLE at AMS

## Abstract

A regression-based approach for temperature data reconstruction using dynamic selection of reconstructing stations and of the coupling period. Applied to fill gaps in automatic temperature records from the meteorological network of Veneto Region in northeastern Italy.

## Key Findings

- Dynamic selection of predictor stations achieves errors near 0C
- Coupling period can precede or follow the missing data
- Outperforms static predictor selection
- Applicable to dense weather station networks

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Recommended gap-filling method for the 50-station network. With dense coverage, regression against nearest correlated stations is the preferred approach for gaps longer than 1-3 days.
