# A Mechanistic Model Simulating Primary Infections of Downy Mildew in Grapevine

- **Authors:** Vittorio Rossi, Tito Caffi, Simona Giosue, Riccardo Bugiani
- **Year:** 2008
- **Journal:** Ecological Modelling, 212(3), 480-491
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ecolmodel.2007.10.046
- **Access:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0304380007005881) (paywalled); [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/248414112)
- **Status:** PAYWALLED - abstract and key findings available

## Abstract

A dynamic model for Plasmopara viticola primary infections on grapevine was developed using a mechanistic approach. Development of the sexual stage of the pathogen was split into different state variables, with changes from one state to another regulated by rates depending on environmental conditions.

## Key Model Description

- Each cohort of oospores progresses along the primary infection cycle simultaneously
- Time step: one hour
- Processes modeled: production and survival of sporangia, release, survival and dispersal of zoospores, infection, appearance of disease symptoms
- Inputs: temperature, leaf wetness, relative humidity, precipitation
- The model accounts for multiple cohorts of oospores maturing at different rates

## Key Findings

- Mechanistic approach successfully simulates primary downy mildew infections
- Model captures the effect of environmental conditions on each stage of the infection process
- Can predict timing and severity of primary infections based on weather data
- Extended by Brischetto et al. (2021) for secondary infections

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

This is one of the core disease models for Direction 2 (spatially explicit disease risk modeling). Running this model across all 50 stations with temperature, leaf wetness, and humidity data produces vineyard-scale downy mildew risk maps. The spatial variation in disease pressure across Tokaj's complex terrain would be a novel contribution for Central European wine regions.
