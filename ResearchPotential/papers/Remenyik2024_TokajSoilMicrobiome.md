# Exploring the Interplay Between the Core Microbiota, Physicochemical Factors, Agrobiochemical Cycles in the Soil of the Historic Tokaj Mad Wine Region

- **Authors:** Janos Remenyik et al.
- **Year:** 2024
- **Journal:** PLoS ONE, 19(4), e0300563
- **DOI:** 10.1371/journal.pone.0300563
- **Access:** Open access at [PLoS ONE](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0300563)
- **Status:** FULL TEXT AVAILABLE

## Abstract

This study examines soil microbiota composition and functioning in historic Hungarian vineyards (Tokaj-Mad) using shotgun metabarcoding. 60 soil samples from four historic dulok (Betsek, Szent Tamas, Kiraly, Nyulaszo) in Mad were analyzed.

## Key Findings

- **Microbial composition:** Three dominant fungal phyla represent ~97% of fungal organisms; bacterial communities show greater overall diversity
- **Soil chemistry-microbiota relationships:** Pronounced shifts in community taxonomy based on soil physicochemical properties; bacterial diversity correlates more strongly with soil mineral content (Mn, Mg)
- **Vineyard differences:** Historic vineyards have distinguishable microbial fingerprints; Szent Tamas shows notably high Bradyrhizobium abundance
- **Seasonal changes:** Spectacular differences in bacterial community diversity between ripening stages; two distinct temporal clusters; fungal communities more stable across the growing season
- **Functional capacity:** Arbuscular mycorrhizae and specific fungal families maintain soil community resilience; soil Na content stimulates N and S metabolism cycles

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Same dulok as in the station dataset (notably Betsek). Microbiome data provides a soil biology dimension that complements the mesoclimatic data from the 50-station network. Could enable soil-climate-microbiome interaction studies specific to Tokaj terroir.
