# Comparing the Use of ERA5 Reanalysis Dataset and Ground-Based Agrometeorological Data Under Different Climates and Topography in Italy

- **Authors:** Daniela Vanella, et al.
- **Year:** 2022
- **Journal:** Journal of Hydrology: Regional Studies
- **DOI:** (ScienceDirect)
- **Access:** [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2214581822001951); [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/362184920)
- **Status:** OPEN ACCESS

## Abstract

Compared ERA5 reanalysis datasets against in-situ agrometeorological observations from 66 automatic weather stations across seven irrigation districts in Italy (2008-2020), distributed under different climate and topography conditions.

## Key Findings

- Temperature RMSE: 1.46C (Bsk climate), 1.70C (Cfa), 1.76C (Csa) in plains
- **RMSE of 2.11-4.84C in mountainous terrain** -- key finding for Tokaj
- Air temperature estimates offered the most accurate reanalysis predictions
- Performance ranking: Tair > RH > Rs > u10
- Systematic biases in complex terrain where sub-grid topography is unresolved

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Directly motivates Manuscript 4 (ERA5 validation). Tokaj's volcanic terrain likely falls in the "problematic range" of 2-5C RMSE identified in mountains. The 50-station network can quantify exactly how much mesoclimatic variability ERA5-Land and FORESEE-HUN miss.
