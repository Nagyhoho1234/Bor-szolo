## Abstract

This definitive review discusses how climate change is affecting grape yield, composition and wine quality. The geography of wine production is changing as a consequence of changing temperature, precipitation, humidity, radiation and CO2 levels. The paper explores consequences for global wine production and adaptation strategies.

## Key Findings (from accessible summaries)

- Central European wine regions identified as potential beneficiaries of warming, with expanding suitability for red varieties
- Comprehensive review of climate change impacts on: yield, berry composition, wine quality, pest/disease pressure
- Adaptation strategies reviewed: cultivar shifts, rootstock selection, canopy management, irrigation, relocation
- Global perspective covering both Old and New World wine regions
- Synthesis of CMIP6/CORDEX projections for viticultural regions
