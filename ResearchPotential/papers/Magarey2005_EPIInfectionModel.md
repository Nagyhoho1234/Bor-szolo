# A Simple Generic Infection Model for Foliar Fungal Plant Pathogens

- **Authors:** Roger D. Magarey, Turner B. Sutton, Clyde L. Thayer
- **Year:** 2005
- **Journal:** Phytopathology, 95(1), 92-100
- **DOI:** 10.1094/PHYTO-95-0092
- **Access:** [APS Journals](https://apsjournals.apsnet.org/doi/10.1094/PHYTO-95-0092); [PDF](https://apsjournals.apsnet.org/doi/pdf/10.1094/PHYTO-95-0092)
- **Status:** ACCESSIBLE (APS Journals)

## Abstract

A simple generic infection model (EPI) for predicting infection periods by fungal foliar pathogens, designed primarily for pathogens lacking extensive epidemiological data. Validated with data from 53 controlled laboratory studies, yielding average correlation coefficient of 0.83 and RMSE of 4.9 hours.

## Key Model Description

- Temperature response function scaled to minimum and optimum wetness duration values
- Minimum wetness duration requirement (Wmin): hours needed to produce 20% disease incidence at a given temperature
- Generic parameterization allowing application to many pathogens
- Inputs: temperature and surface wetness duration

## Key Findings

- Generic model performs comparably to pathogen-specific models when detailed data is lacking
- R = 0.83 across 53 validation studies
- Applicable to downy mildew, powdery mildew, and other foliar diseases
- Leaf wetness sensors require local calibration (paint-type vs impedance-type respond differently)

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

An alternative to the Rossi et al. (2008) model for downy mildew risk assessment, especially useful as a simplified approach applicable across the 50-station network. The generic nature allows application to multiple disease systems simultaneously.
