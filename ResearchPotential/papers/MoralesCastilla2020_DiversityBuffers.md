# Diversity Buffers Winegrowing Regions from Climate Change Losses

- **Authors:** Ignacio Morales-Castilla, Inaki Garcia de Cortazar-Atauri, Benjamin I. Cook, Thierry Lacombe, Amber Parker, Cornelis van Leeuwen, Kimberly A. Nicholas, Elizabeth M. Wolkovich
- **Year:** 2020
- **Journal:** Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 117(6), 2864-2869
- **DOI:** 10.1073/pnas.1906731117
- **Access:** Open access at [PNAS](https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.1906731117) and [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7022210/)
- **Status:** FULL TEXT AVAILABLE

## Abstract

Cultivar diversity more than halved projected losses of current winegrowing areas under a 2C warming scenario, decreasing areas lost from 56% to 24%. At 4C warming, benefits diminished but still provided meaningful protection, reducing losses by one-third.

## Key Findings

- **Without cultivar adaptation:** 51% of climatically suitable areas lost at 2C; 77% at 4C
- **With cultivar turnover:** Losses decline to 24% at 2C (57% improvement); 58% at 4C (32% improvement)
- Later-ripening varieties (Grenache, Monastrell) essential for maintaining existing production regions
- Early-ripening cultivars (Pinot noir, Chasselas) dominate newly suitable northern territories
- Spain and Italy projected to lose 65-68% of suitable areas even with adaptation at 2C
- France shows more balanced impacts (22% loss, 25% gain at 2C)

## Methodology

- 11 globally planted varieties studied (35% of global plantings)
- Nonlinear process-based sequential phenological models (Smoothed-Utah + Wang-Engel)
- Historical phenological observations from 62 French locations, 1956-2015
- NCAR Large Ensemble (CESM) climate projections at ~1 degree resolution
- Suitability assessed via bioclimatic envelopes fitted to 1950-1980 conditions

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Demonstrates the critical importance of cultivar diversity for climate adaptation. Tokaj's reliance on Furmint, Harslevelyu, and Sarga Muskotaly represents a narrow genetic base that could be vulnerable. The methodology for assessing variety-specific suitability windows applies directly to the 50-station network data.
