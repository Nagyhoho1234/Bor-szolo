## Summary

Airborne hyperspectral imagery covering the entire Tokaj Wine Region. High-resolution spectral-spatial geodata analyzed for variety determination, biophysical properties (NDVI, LAI, Red Edge Position), canopy continuity, structure, and row anomalies.

## Key Details

- Evaluation area: entire Tokaj wine region (~11,000 ha), September 2014
- Airborne LiDAR (LEICA ALS70HP) + SWIR hyperspectral imagery (1000-2450 nm)
- Biophysical indices: NDVI, LAI, Red Edge Position
- Large-scale topography, soil, and meso/macroclimate characterization
- Institutions: Corvinus University Budapest, Tokaj Kereskedohaz, MTA soil science, University of Leipzig

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Sentinel-2 NDVI at 10 m resolution can be correlated with station-derived climate indices. This existing hyperspectral dataset provides a high-resolution vegetation response baseline for Tokaj that can be linked to the 50-station climate data.
