# About Regression-Kriging: From Equations to Case Studies

- **Authors:** Tomislav Hengl, Gerard B.M. Heuvelink, David G. Rossiter
- **Year:** 2007
- **Journal:** Computers and Geosciences, 33, 1301-1315 (also earlier: Hengl et al. 2004, Geoderma, 120, 75-93)
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.cageo.2007.05.001
- **Access:** [Wageningen University](https://research.wur.nl/en/publications/about-regression-kriging-from-equations-to-case-studies); [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/222521073)
- **Status:** PAYWALLED at publisher; preprints may be available

## Abstract

Formalizes regression-kriging (RK) as a spatial interpolation technique combining regression on auxiliary variables with simple kriging of regression residuals. Proves mathematical equivalence with Universal Kriging (UK) and Kriging with External Drift (KED).

## Key Contributions

- RK, UK, and KED yield exactly the same predictions if using same inputs and same geostatistical model
- Advantage of RK: ability to extend to broader range of regression techniques
- Allows separate interpretation of trend and residual components
- Practical guidelines for implementation
- Case studies demonstrating application

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

The theoretical basis for the regression-kriging workflow in Manuscript 1. The equivalence proof means the choice between RK and KED/UK is a practical/computational one, not a statistical one. RK is preferred because it allows testing alternative regression methods (e.g., SVR from Le Roux et al. 2017).
