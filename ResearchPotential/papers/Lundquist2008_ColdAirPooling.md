# Automated Algorithm for Mapping Regions of Cold-Air Pooling in Complex Terrain

- **Authors:** Jessica D. Lundquist, Nicholas Pepin, Caitlin Rochford
- **Year:** 2008
- **Journal:** Journal of Geophysical Research, 113, D22107
- **DOI:** 10.1029/2008JD009879
- **Access:** [AGU/Wiley](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1029/2008JD009879) (paywalled); [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/52009277)
- **Status:** PAYWALLED - abstract available

## Abstract

In complex terrain, air cooled by radiative energy loss on calm, clear nights sinks to valley bottoms as cold air drainage. This paper presents an automated algorithm for mapping regions susceptible to cold-air pooling using DEM-derived topographic variables.

## Key Findings

- Elevation and slope steepness are primary drivers of cold air drainage and frost risk distribution
- Automated mapping algorithm identifies cold-air pools from DEM data
- Tested across three geographic locations: Rocky Mountain National Park, Pyrenees, Yosemite
- Topographic Position Index (TPI) and flow accumulation are key predictive variables
- Clear, calm nights (radiation frost conditions) produce the strongest cold pooling

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Foundational reference for Direction 3 (spring frost risk mapping). The automated algorithm can be applied to Tokaj's DEM to map cold-air pooling susceptibility, then validated against the 50-station minimum temperature observations.
