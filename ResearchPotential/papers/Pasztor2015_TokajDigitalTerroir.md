## Abstract

The research addresses sustainable quality wine production in Tokaj, Europe's first classified wine region. The study characterized viticultural potential across the region's 11,000+ hectares spanning 27 villages.

## Key Methodology

- Four primary data sources: airborne laser scanning (LiDAR), hyperspectral imagery, digital soil mapping, interpolated climate data
- 25-meter operational spatial resolution
- LiDAR data acquired using LEICA ALS70HP on Cessna TU-206E, average point density 4 per m2, RMSE=15 cm
- Calculated metrics: plant available water content, vigor potential, precocity (earliness)
- Enabled determination of optimal production targets (dessert wine, dry wine, sparkling wine)

## Key Findings

- Soil plays a dominant role in determining viticultural potential and terroir delineation
- Most relevant soil properties: drainage, water holding capacity, soil depth, pH
- Combined LiDAR + hyperspectral + soil mapping provides comprehensive terroir characterization
- 25 m resolution achievable for vineyard-scale mapping

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

This is the most comprehensive existing digital terroir map of Tokaj. The critical gap it identified is **in-situ mesoclimatic data** -- all climate characterization relied on interpolated legacy records or coarse gridded products. The 50-station network fills exactly this gap.
