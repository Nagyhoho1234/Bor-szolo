# A Multicriteria Climatic Classification System for Grape-Growing Regions Worldwide

## Abstract

This paper establishes the Multicriteria Climatic Classification (MCC) system for grape-growing regions worldwide. The system combines three synthetic and complementary viticultural climate indices: the Huglin Index (HI, heliothermal conditions), the Cool Night Index (CI, night coolness during maturation), and the Dryness Index (DI, soil water balance). The classification was applied to 97 grape-growing regions across 29 countries.

## Key Contributions

- Defined three bioclimatic indices (HI, CI, DI) as the foundation for viticultural climate classification
- HI classes: Very cool (HI <= 1500), Cool (1500-1800), Temperate (1800-2100), Warm temperate (2100-2400), Warm (2400-3000), Very warm (HI > 3000)
- CI classes: Very cool nights (CI <= 12C), Cool nights (12-14C), Temperate nights (14-18C), Warm nights (CI > 18C)
- DI classes: Very dry (DI <= -200mm), Moderately dry (-200 to -100mm), Sub-humid (-100 to -50mm), Humid (DI > -50mm)
- Applied PCA to 97 regions showing clear clustering patterns
- Established the framework used by subsequent terroir and climate zoning studies worldwide

## Methodology

- Huglin Index: Sum of (Tmean - 10) + (Tmax - 10) / 2 multiplied by a day-length coefficient, accumulated April-September (Northern Hemisphere)
- Cool Night Index: Mean minimum temperature during September (month of ripening)
- Dryness Index: Climatic water balance using Penman-type potential evapotranspiration, April-September
- PCA + hierarchical clustering applied to standardized index values for 97 regions

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

This is the foundational framework for Direction 1 (mesoclimatic terroir zoning). Computing HI, CI, and DI at all 50 stations and applying PCA + clustering directly follows this methodology.
