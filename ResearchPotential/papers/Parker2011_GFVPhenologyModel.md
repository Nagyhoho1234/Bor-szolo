# General Phenological Model to Characterise the Timing of Flowering and Veraison of Vitis vinifera L.

- **Authors:** Amber K. Parker, Inaki Garcia de Cortazar-Atauri, Cornelis van Leeuwen, Isabelle Chuine
- **Year:** 2011
- **Journal:** Australian Journal of Grape and Wine Research, 17(2), 206-216
- **DOI:** 10.1111/j.1755-0238.2011.00140.x
- **Access:** [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/229985696) (paywalled at Wiley)
- **Status:** PAYWALLED - abstract and key findings available

## Abstract

The GFV (Grapevine Flowering Veraison) model was developed to select the simplest model that gave the best trade-off between parsimony and efficiency for predicting flowering and veraison timing for V. vinifera L. The model was calibrated and validated using a database of 81 varieties, 2,278 flowering observations and 2,088 veraison observations, spanning 1960-2007 from 123 locations (predominantly France).

## Key Model Description

- **Type:** Forcing-only thermal time model
- **Base temperature:** 0C
- **Start date:** DOY 60 (March 1)
- **Cultivar-specific parameter:** F* (thermal threshold for flowering/veraison)
- **Input required:** Daily Tmin and Tmax (to derive Tmean)
- **Output:** Predicted dates of flowering and veraison

## Key Findings

- Simple forcing-only model outperformed more complex dormancy+forcing models for most varieties
- Successfully parameterized for 81+ varieties
- **Furmint and Harslevelyu have NOT been parameterized** - clear publication opportunity
- Model is simple for users and can predict phenology under climate change scenarios

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

This is the core framework for Manuscript 5 (phenology model calibration). Furmint and Harslevelyu have never been parameterized for the GFV model, representing a genuine gap. The 50-station gradient allows testing whether a single F* value works across all mesoclimatic zones or whether spatial calibration is needed.
