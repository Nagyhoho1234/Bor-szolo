# A Weather-Driven Model for Predicting Infections of Grapevines by Sporangia of Plasmopara viticola

- **Authors:** Chiara Brischetto, Federica Bove, Giorgia Fedele, Vittorio Rossi
- **Year:** 2021
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Plant Science, 12, 636607
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fpls.2021.636607
- **Access:** Open access at [Frontiers](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/plant-science/articles/10.3389/fpls.2021.636607/full) and [PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7985336/)
- **Status:** FULL TEXT AVAILABLE

## Abstract

This paper extends Rossi et al. (2008) to predict secondary Plasmopara viticola infections. The mechanistic model addresses sporangia production and survival on downy mildew lesions, dispersal and deposition, and infection based on weather conditions.

## Key Results

- Correctly predicted absence of sporangia conditions with 67% posterior probability
- Only 3% of vineyard sporangia missed when not predicted
- Accurately identified 39 of 40 observed sporangia peaks
- Predicted non-infection periods with 87% accuracy, missing only 9 of 108 real infections
- Model enables transition from calendar-based to risk-based fungicide schedules

## Methodology

- Begins when initial seasonal downy mildew lesions are detected through vineyard scouting
- Models environmental conditions favorable for secondary infection periods
- Assesses relative infection severity without predicting disease progression curves
- Three years of vineyard observation data for validation

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Extends the Rossi et al. (2008) primary infection model to secondary infections. Combined, these models provide a complete downy mildew risk assessment framework applicable across the 50-station network.
