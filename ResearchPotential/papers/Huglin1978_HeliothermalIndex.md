## Summary

This seminal paper introduced the Huglin Heliothermal Index (HI), a bioclimatic heat index for viticulture regions. The index calculates the temperature sum above 10C from April through September (Northern Hemisphere), using both daily maximum and average temperature, with a latitude-based day-length correction coefficient.

## The Huglin Index Formula

HI = Sum from April 1 to Sept 30 of: [(Tmean - 10) + (Tmax - 10)] / 2 * d

Where d is a day-length coefficient ranging from 1.02 to 1.06 depending on latitude.

## Classification

- Very cool: HI <= 1500
- Cool: 1500-1800
- Temperate: 1800-2100
- Warm temperate: 2100-2400
- Warm: 2400-3000
- Very warm: HI > 3000

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

One of the three core indices in the Tonietto-Carbonneau MCC system. Computing HI at all 50 stations is a fundamental step for Direction 1 (mesoclimatic zoning). Historic classification paper that underpins all subsequent viticultural climate analysis.
