# The Concept of Terroir in Viticulture

- **Authors:** Cornelis van Leeuwen, Gerard Seguin
- **Year:** 2006
- **Journal:** Journal of Wine Research, 17(1), 1-10
- **DOI:** 10.1080/09571260600633135
- **Access:** [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/236902064) (paywalled at Taylor & Francis)
- **Status:** PAYWALLED - abstract available

## Abstract

Terroir is defined as an interactive cultivated ecosystem, in a given place, including climate, soil and the vine. This paper establishes the conceptual framework for terroir as a complex system where environmental factors interact with cultivation practices and genetic material.

## Key Contributions

- Formal definition of terroir as interactive cultivated ecosystem
- Framework for understanding climate-soil-cultivar interactions
- Foundation for quantitative terroir studies
- Widely cited in subsequent viticultural research

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Provides the conceptual basis for interpreting results from the 50-station network in the context of Tokaj's terroir. The "interactive ecosystem" definition frames how mesoclimatic data interacts with soil and variety factors.
