## Abstract

This study predicts drought periods affecting the Tokaj-Hegyalja wine region using daily precipitation and daily maximum temperature data from the Hungarian Meteorological Service (OMSZ) for 2002-2020. The Markov model was applied as a mathematical method for statistical investigation of weather phenomena.

## Key Findings

- Over a 19-year period, the average P2 limiting probability is 20.8%
- This means there is a 20.8% chance of any day during the vegetation season (March 1 to October 31) falling within a drought period
- Markov chains are valuable for organizing integrated pest management
- Can be used to plan irrigation, control fungal pathogens, and plan vintage success
- Markov chains are more efficient for short-term forecasts
- Applicability reduced for long-term forecasts, especially under climate change

## Methodology

- Data source: OMSZ daily precipitation and Tmax, 2002-2020
- Markov chain transition probabilities for drought classification
- Vegetation season analysis (March-October)

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Provides the only published drought analysis specific to Tokaj using OMSZ records. The 50-station network can extend this with spatially distributed drought analysis, revealing how drought risk varies across the volcanic landscape. Connects to Direction 5 (vineyard water balance and drought stress).
