# Development of an Infection Model for Botrytis Bunch Rot of Grapes Based on Wetness Duration and Temperature

- **Authors:** J.C. Broome, J.T. English, J.J. Marois, B.A. Latorre, J.C. Aviles
- **Year:** 1995
- **Journal:** Phytopathology, 85, 97-102
- **DOI:** N/A (pre-DOI or early DOI)

## Abstract

Empirical model expressing Botrytis cinerea infection severity on grape flowers and berries as a function of wetness duration and temperature. One of the foundational empirical models for grey mould prediction.

## Key Model Description

- Inputs: leaf wetness duration and temperature
- Output: infection severity on flowers and/or berries
- Empirical response surface relating wetness-temperature combinations to infection probability
- Developed from controlled environment and field studies

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Provides an alternative/complementary approach to the Gonzalez-Dominguez (2015) mechanistic model for Botrytis assessment. Simpler to implement across the 50-station network but less mechanistically detailed.
