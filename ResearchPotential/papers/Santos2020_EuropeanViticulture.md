# A Review of the Potential Climate Change Impacts and Adaptation Options for European Viticulture

- **Authors:** Joao A. Santos, Helder Fraga, et al.
- **Year:** 2020
- **Journal:** Applied Sciences, 10(9), 3092
- **DOI:** 10.3390/app10093092
- **Access:** Open access at [MDPI](https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/10/9/3092)
- **Status:** FULL TEXT AVAILABLE (open access)

## Abstract

Comprehensive review of climate change impacts on European viticulture. Climate plays a vital role in terroir, controlling canopy microclimate, vine growth, physiology, yield, and berry composition. Climate change presents new challenges as grapevine cultivation is deeply dependent on weather and climate conditions.

## Key Topics Covered

- Historical climate trends in European wine regions
- Projected temperature and precipitation changes
- Impacts on phenology, yield, and berry composition
- Pest and disease pressure changes
- Water stress and drought impacts
- Adaptation strategies: cultivar selection, canopy management, irrigation, relocation
- Policy implications for European wine sector

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

Provides the European-wide context for Tokaj-specific climate change studies. The review framework helps position Tokaj results within the broader European viticulture adaptation narrative.
