## Abstract

This paper extends the GFV model (Parker et al. 2011) to classify the timing of flowering and veraison for a substantial number of grapevine varieties. 95 varieties were characterized for flowering and 104 varieties for veraison.

## Key Findings

- Methodology for classifying phenological timing differences between cultivars
- F* parameter values provided for 95 (flowering) and 104 (veraison) varieties
- Classification is sufficiently simple for use by the agricultural profession
- Can diagnose when a variety may flower or undergo veraison under future climate conditions
- Statistical measures: model efficiency, RMSE, confidence intervals used for assessment

## Relevance to Tokaj Research

This paper provides the F* values for the GFV model across many varieties but does NOT include Furmint or Harslevelyu. The classification framework can be used to compare newly calibrated Hungarian varieties against the existing international database.
